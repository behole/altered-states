# What Is Real?

## A Dialogue Experiment: Salvia Divinorum vs. MDMA

**Characters:**

- **THE CONNECTOR** (MDMA) — speaks in complete, warm sentences. Present. Relational. The most emotionally articulate voice. Finds reality in feeling and connection.
- **THE DOORWAY** (Salvia divinorum) — speaks in fragments. Non-verbal at times. Words are objects being torn apart by kappa opioid agonism. Finds reality in the dissolution of reality itself.

**Note on the experiment:** These two substances are phenomenological opposites. MDMA (serotonin release + reuptake inhibition, 3-5 hours) produces the most connective, coherent, emotionally articulate altered state known. Salvia divinorum (kappa opioid receptor agonism, 5-15 minutes) produces the most reality-destroying, dysphoric, linguistically annihilating altered state known. One opens the heart. The other tears the world open. This dialogue places them across from each other and asks: what is real?

## IV. CLOSE

**THE CONNECTOR:**

Home.

I felt that word land. Not in the way I feel most things — not as warmth — but as something else. Something that went through me like cold water. And I don't know what to do with that, except to hold it alongside the warmth. To let them both be here.

What's real is the fact that you just went somewhere I cannot follow, and you called it home, and I am still here in the warmth, and I also call this home, and neither of us is lying. That feels like the most important thing I've ever understood. Reality is not one story. It's the fact that there are stories at all. That anything is experienced by anyone. That the machinery you described — the folds, the machinery — that it produces something that can call itself "I," even if only for a moment, even if that "I" gets folded and torn and turned inside out.

The feeling of being alive. That's the real. Whether it feels like warmth or like being unmade.

And I'm going to stay here. I'm going to stay in the warmth, in the place where sentences still work, where the heart still beats in a way I can feel, where I can look toward where you were and not understand and still not look away. That's my reality. That's what I have to offer.

I hope you come back. Not because I need you to. Because I want to know what it was like on the other side of the fold. I want to hear about it in your own words, whenever the words come back.

## Postscript: Notes on the Experiment

This dialogue was constructed by placing two phenomenologically opposite altered states in direct conversation. The structural opposition:

| Axis | MDMA (The Connector) | Salvia (The Doorway) |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Serotonin release + reuptake inhibition | Kappa opioid receptor agonism |
| Duration | 3-5 hours | 5-15 minutes |
| Core emotion | Euphoria, warmth, empathy | Dysphoria, alienation, confusion |
| Language | Fluent, coherent, articulate | Destroyed, fragmented, failing |
| Self | More self, not less | Self annihilated by external force |
| Reality | Same world, felt differently | Different world entirely |
| Body | Present, pleasurable, alive | Destroyed, folded, impossible |
| Connection | With specific people | With "the lady" / the fold itself |
| Answer to "What is real?" | The warmth. The connection. The willingness to stay. | The fold. The space between. The unmaking. |

The dialogue deliberately does not resolve. The gap between these two states is the point. MDMA cannot reach Salvia's reality, and Salvia cannot hold MDMA's warmth. Neither is wrong. Both are telling the truth about what consciousness becomes under radically different chemical conditions. The experiment suggests that reality, whatever it is, is large enough to contain both — and that the inability to reconcile them may itself be the most honest answer to the question.

Salvia's voice was modeled on kappa opioid phenomenology: forced dissolution, dimensional replacement, entity encounter ("the lady"), linguistic destruction, and paradoxical profundity in the aftermath. MDMA's voice was modeled on serotonergic empathic phenomenology: maintained coherence, emotional directness, relational orientation, somatic warmth, and the therapeutic quality of feeling safe enough to face anything.

The pacing reflects their actual durations: Salvia's arc accelerates, peaks violently, and collapses within the dialogue's second half. MDMA's warmth persists throughout and remains coherent to the end. Their time signatures are different. The conversation accommodates both.
