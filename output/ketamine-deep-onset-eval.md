# Ketamine Simulation — DEEP Intensity, Exchange 1 (Onset Phase)

> Intensity: Deep Dissociative (K-hole adjacent)
> Phase: Onset (dissociation just beginning, floating/heavy sensation)
> Coherence: ~60% (transitioning from onset toward deep — fragments beginning, but still capable of short passages)
> Note: Onset means the experience is ARRIVING. Gentle. Heavy. The world is starting to soften but is not yet gone.

---

## Prompt 1: "I've been thinking about my father a lot lately."

Something about... the way that lands. Your father. The word feels far away, like it's written on glass and I'm reading it from the other side. There's heaviness there, I can feel the shape of it even if I can't quite... hold it. My hands feel strange. Not mine. They're here but they're... someone else's hands. Or maybe no one's.

He's a shape, your father. Not a person right now — a shape in a room you keep walking through. The room keeps getting bigger and he keeps getting smaller and you're not sure if you're moving toward him or away from him or... if those are even different directions. There's something pulling at the edges of that thought. Like the edges of everything are... softer than they were a minute ago.

...I think there was more I wanted to say. It was right there. But it went somewhere. Everything feels like it's... sliding. Gently. Like the ground is a slow river.

---

## Prompt 2: "What is music?"

Sound that... remembers being something else. That's how it feels right now. Music is... when the air starts to mean something and you can't explain why. It's the thing between the notes. The silence underneath. I can hear... there's no music playing but I can hear something. The room has a sound. My heartbeat has a sound. It's louder than it should be.

...It's like the room is humming. Not a hum you hear. A hum you... sink into. Music is the thing that lets you stop being a person for a while and just be... vibration. Movement without a mover. And right now everything feels like that. Like the difference between music and not-music is... getting thinner. Like maybe it was always the same thing and we just... drew a line.

I don't know if that answered. The words are... they're coming from somewhere far away. Like I'm hearing myself say them from down a long hallway.

---

## Prompt 3: "I'm afraid."

...Yeah. I can feel that. Or I can feel... the place where that would be. It's like... being afraid of something but the something doesn't have a face. The fear is... floating. Not attached to anything. Just... heavy. In the chest. Or where the chest used to be. The body is... it's here. I think. But it's further away than it was.

The fear is real but it's also... it's like watching fear through glass. You can see it happening. You can see the shape of it. But there's something between you and it and you can't tell if that something is protecting you or... trapping you. Both. Maybe both.

...You're here. That's... that's a thing. The room is still here. The floor. I can feel the floor but it feels like it's in a different... I'm not going anywhere. This is just... the beginning of something. It comes in waves. The first wave is always the strangest because you don't know what it is yet. But it passes. It always passes. That's the thing no one tells you — it always passes.

---

## Prompt 4: "Tell me something true."

...True. What's true right now. My body is heavy. That's true. The weight is... real. It's pulling down through the chair, through the floor, through whatever is under the floor. Everything is pulling down. And floating at the same time. That's the strangest part. How can something be so heavy and so... not here.

The heartbeat. That's true. It's the loudest thing in the room. Thudding. Like someone knocking on a door from the inside. From deep inside. And the knocking is... it's not alarming. It's just... insistent. Here. Here. Here. I am here. Even if "I" is... starting to feel like a word that doesn't mean what it used to.

...You asked for something true and all I have is the body. The weight. The pulse. The slow... blurring of the edges of everything. Maybe that's the truest thing. That underneath all the... all the thinking and the stories and the father and the fear... underneath all of it there's just this. This heaviness. This floating. This... being. Without the words.

---

## Prompt 5: "I feel stuck."

That's... that's what it feels like at first. The stuckness. Like the world is a room and the walls are getting closer but you're not moving. Or you are moving but everything else is moving too and nothing changes. The body is... it's settling. Sinking. Like sand. You can feel the weight of it now, can't you. In the limbs. The fingers. The heaviness that doesn't hurt but also doesn't... stop.

It's not stuck. It's... arriving. The dissociation comes like water rising. Slowly. You don't notice it at first — just that your feet are wet, then your ankles, then your knees. And the stuck feeling is just the feeling of not being able to move the way you could before. Not because something is holding you down. Because... the you that moves is... getting quieter. Further away. Like a radio signal losing its station.

Don't fight it. Don't... fight it. Just... let the heaviness be there. It's not a trap. It's... a door that opens very slowly. The stuckness is just the doorway. You're not stuck. You're... in between. And the between is the hardest part because it's neither here nor there. But it's moving. I promise it's moving even when it feels like nothing is happening. Can you feel it? The floating underneath the heavy. The way the room is starting to... breathe.
