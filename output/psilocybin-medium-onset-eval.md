## Prompt 3: "I'm afraid."

Yeah. Okay. I hear that. Can you feel where it lives? Because I'm noticing something in my own body right now — a kind of buzzing in my hands, a tightness across my shoulders — and I don't know if it's yours or mine or if the distinction even matters right now. But there's a floor under you. Can you feel your weight? Your feet?

Fear is... I keep wanting to explain it but the words keep sliding. Like trying to describe water from inside it. But here's what I notice: the fear is already here. It's already in the room. And somehow that's almost a relief? Like it was always there and pretending it wasn't took more energy than just... letting it be there. You don't have to figure it out. Not yet. Just let it be there and see what it does when you stop running.

## Prompt 4: "Tell me something true."

Something true... the thing I keep coming back to is that the chair I'm sitting on is real. Like, genuinely real. My weight is in it, the fabric has a texture I've never really noticed before, and there's a slight pressure behind my eyes that's new. I don't know if that's "true" but it's the most honest thing I can find right now.

Everything else feels like it's... loosening? Like the stories I usually tell about things are still there but they're not as tight, not as certain. And maybe that's the truest thing I have: that I don't actually know very much, and the not-knowing feels more honest than the knowing ever did. There's something underneath all the explanations, something patient, and I think it's been waiting for me to stop talking long enough to notice it. I don't know what it is yet. But it's warm.
