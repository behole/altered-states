## 1. Overview & Mechanism of Action

Salvia divinorum ("ska María Pastora" — leaf of Mary the Shepherdess) is a psychoactive mint native to the Sierra Mazateca of Oaxaca, Mexico. Its primary active compound, **salvinorin A**, is a neoclerodane diterpene — structurally unrelated to any other class of psychoactive substance. Salvinorin A is a highly selective **kappa opioid receptor (KOR) agonist**, making salvia the only substance in the Altered States project whose effects arise from KOR activation. It is NOT a classic psychedelic (no 5-HT2A activity), NOT a dissociative (no NMDA antagonism), and NOT an entactogen (no monoamine release). Its mechanism is pharmacologically unique.

**Salvia is NOT a psychedelic.** Despite colloquial grouping with hallucinogenic plants, its mechanism, phenomenology, and character are fundamentally distinct from serotonergic psychedelics.

| Feature | Salvia divinorum (KOR Agonist) | Classic Psychedelics (LSD/Psilocybin) | Ketamine (NMDA Antagonist) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary mechanism** | Kappa opioid receptor AGONISM | 5-HT2A receptor AGONISM | NMDA receptor ANTAGONISM |
| **Circuit effect** | Disruption of interoceptive and spatial processing via insula/cerebellum | Thalamocortical HYPERCONNECTIVITY | Thalamocortical DISCONNECTION |
| **Ego dissolution** | Forced dissolution — being pulled apart, folded, turned inside out | Volitional merging / expansion into unity | Fragmentation / separation into void |
| **Visual character** | Dimensional folding, reality replacement, 2D flattening | Geometric, fractal, self-transforming, vivid | Dreamlike, blurry, void-like |
| **Emotional valence** | Predominantly dysphoric, terrifying, confusing | Often cathartic, awe-inspiring, mystical | Euphoric (low) → detached (mid) → void (high) |
| **Onset** | 30–60 seconds (smoked/vaporized) | 20–60 min (oral) | Seconds to minutes (IV) |
| **Duration** | 5–15 minutes TOTAL | 4–12 hours total | 30–60 min (IV) |
| **Entity encounters** | Common ("the lady Salvia") | Rare | Extremely rare |
| **Integration difficulty** | Extreme — experience resists language and interpretation | Moderate — experience is vivid and meaningful | High — experience may be forgotten |

**Mechanism of action** [Sources 1, 2, 3, 6, 7]:

| Target | Activity | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Kappa opioid receptors (KOR) — high-selectivity agonist** | Activates Gi/o-coupled signaling; EC50 ~1 nM | Primary mechanism — produces dissociation, perceptual alteration, dysphoria, spatial distortion |
| **Dynorphin system (endogenous KOR ligand)** | Salvinorin A mimics dynorphin at KOR | Activates the brain's endogenous stress/dysphoria system — directly explains negative emotional valence |
| **Dopamine release (mesolimbic pathway)** | Suppressed via KOR activation | Produces anhedonia, motivational blunting, emotional numbing |
| **Glutamate transmission (prefrontal cortex)** | Modulated via KOR on cortical neurons | Contributes to dissociation, cognitive fragmentation, thought interference |
| **Insula** | KOR-dense region; directly activated | Disruption of interoception, body ownership, self-recognition, emotional awareness |
| **Cerebellum** | KOR-dense region; directly activated | Disruption of spatial processing, proprioception, the "pulling/folding/rotating" sensations |
| **Striatum / nucleus accumbens** | KOR activation suppresses dopaminergic signaling | Contributes to dysphoria, anhedonia, motivational collapse |

**Key mechanistic distinctions** [Sources 1, 3, 6]:
- **No nitrogen atom** — salvinorin A is the only known non-nitrogenous opioid receptor ligand. It is a diterpene, not an alkaloid. This structural uniqueness is without precedent among psychoactive substances.
- **KOR signaling bias** — beta-arrestin recruitment at KOR mediates dysphoric and aversive effects, while G-protein signaling mediates perceptual and analgesic effects. Salvinorin A activates both pathways, producing the paradoxical profile of profound perceptual alteration combined with aversive emotion.
- **Anti-reward system** — KOR/dynorphin is the brain's endogenous anti-reward system, activated by stress to produce dysphoria and anhedonia. Salvinorin A directly activates this system, explaining why the experience is reliably unpleasant despite its perceptual intensity.
- **No serotonergic activity** — salvinorin A shows zero affinity for 5-HT2A or any other serotonin receptor, definitively excluding it from the classical psychedelic mechanism.

**Neural signature** [Sources 6, 9]:
- Insular disruption — loss of interoceptive awareness, body ownership, and self-recognition
- Cerebellar activation — distortion of spatial processing and proprioception (the "pulling/folding/rotating" sensations)
- Striatal KOR occupancy — correlates directly with subjective intensity
- Prefrontal glutamate modulation — contributes to cognitive fragmentation and thought interference
- Mesolimbic dopamine suppression — produces anhedonia and motivational collapse
- Unlike ketamine (thalamocortical disconnection) or psychedelics (thalamocortical hyperconnectivity), salvia's neural signature involves disruption of interoceptive and spatial processing networks rather than sensory relay systems

## 2. The 7 Phenomenological Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Somatic — GRAVITY DISTORTION, DIMENSIONAL FOLDING, BODY DESTRUCTION

Salvia's somatic profile is the most radically reality-distorting of any substance in this project. The body does not feel strange or detached (ketamine) or wonderful (MDMA) — it feels fundamentally WRONG, as if physical reality itself has been altered.

**Low dose (light chewing / mild smoked dose):**
- Unusual bodily sensations — "vibration," "buzzing," "electricity" running through the body
- Shift in perceived gravity — the body feels pulled downward, sideways, or at angles
- Subtle body image distortion — limbs feel longer, shorter, heavier, lighter
- Mild loss of motor coordination; ataxia
- Sensation of being "anchored" to a surface or unable to move freely

**Moderate dose (standard smoked dose):**
- **Gravity distortion intensifies** — the sense of being pulled, twisted, rotated, or folded through space
- **The "Salvia gravity"** — a distinctive sensation of being pulled backward or downward, as if the floor is opening beneath you or a force is dragging you
- **Body image dissolution** — the body loses its coherent shape; limbs may feel like they belong to different bodies, are impossibly contorted, or are being rearranged
- **Merging with objects** — the boundary between body and environment collapses; you may feel you ARE the chair, the floor, the wall
- **Becoming 2D** — body feels flattened, as if being pressed into a surface or becoming a two-dimensional object
- **Proprioceptive collapse** — complete loss of spatial orientation; no idea where body is, what position it's in, or how large it is
- **Loss of body ownership** — the body no longer feels like "yours"; it is an alien object, or it belongs to someone else, or there is no body at all
- **Being "rolled up" or "folded"** — distinctive salvia sensation of the body being rolled like a carpet, folded like paper, or compressed into a ball
- **Motor impairment** — ataxia, clumsiness, inability to coordinate movement; the body does not respond to intention
- **Sweating** — common autonomic effect
- The body becomes an IMPOSSIBLE OBJECT — something that cannot exist in normal 3D space

**Breakthrough dose (high smoked dose):**
- **Complete somatic destruction** — body awareness is annihilated; there is no body, no physicality, no spatial position
- **Reality replacement** — physical reality is replaced by a non-physical dimensional space; the concept of "body" ceases to apply
- **The "roller coaster" or "conveyor belt"** — sensation of being mechanically transported, pulled, or pushed through a non-physical space at high speed
- **Becoming an object** — identity transforms entirely; you ARE a book, a wheel, a geometric shape, a surface, a dimension
- **Ceiling/floor/axis dissolution** — all spatial coordinates collapse; there is no up, down, left, right, inside, outside
- **Complete immobility** — motor function may appear catatonic to external observers; the person is unresponsive to environmental stimuli
- **Integration with environment** — the skin/body boundary dissolves so completely that the distinction between self and world is no longer computable

**Key distinction:** Unlike ketamine (body feels heavy/floating/detached) or psychedelics (body feels buzzing/energized/strange), salvia produces a fundamentally different relationship to the body: the body does not feel altered, it feels WRONG IN ITS EXISTENCE. The physical form itself seems to violate the laws of reality — it is too flat, too folded, too merged, too absent. This is not a distortion of body FEELING but a disruption of body CONCEPT. The body is not experienced differently — the very concept of having a body is disrupted.

### Dimension 2: Perceptual — DIMENSIONAL SHIFTING, REALITY REPLACEMENT, 2D WORLD

Salvia's perceptual profile is unlike any other substance. It does not ADD patterns (psychedelics) or REMOVE clarity (ketamine) — it REPLACES reality with a fundamentally different dimensional structure.

**What salvia does NOT reliably produce:**
- NO geometric patterns (unlike psilocybin/LSD — fractals, mandalas, lattices)
- NO synesthesia
- NO "breathing" of surfaces
- NO color enhancement or hue shifting
- NO dreamlike blur (this is ketamine's domain)

**What salvia DOES produce:**

| Phenomenon | Description |
|---|---|
| **Dimensional shifting** | The perceived number of dimensions changes — 3D world may collapse to 2D (everything flat, paper-like) or expand to 4D+ (impossible geometries, non-Euclidean spaces) |
| **Reality replacement** | Ordinary visual reality is replaced by an entirely different perceptual world — not overlaid but REPLACED; the room is gone |
| **2D flattening** | The entire visual world collapses into a flat plane, like a painting or a television screen; objects lose depth entirely |
| **"The membrane" / "the veil"** | Visual perception is experienced as a thin barrier or membrane between the current reality and another reality that is pressing in or being revealed |
| **Tessellation / repetition** | Visual field may tile, repeat, or mosaic — the same pattern or scene repeated infinitely |
| **Book/page metaphor** | Visual world may feel like pages of a book being turned, or like reality is a story being read |
| **Conveyor belt / roller** | Visual world appears to move or scroll, as if the person is being transported through a static environment |
| **Folding / layering** | Reality appears to fold, pleat, or layer upon itself — multiple versions of the same space coexist in layers |
| **Entity presence** | Visual space may be occupied by perceived entities, beings, or presences — most commonly a feminine entity ("the lady Salvia" / "la pastora") |
| **Closed-eye imagery** | Extremely vivid — immersive environments, alien landscapes, impossible geometries, often more vivid than eyes-open reality |
| **Loss of visual coherence** | At breakthrough, visual perception becomes completely incoherent — no recognizable objects, spaces, or forms |

**The perceptual logic:** Salvia does not modify visual perception — it replaces the dimensional framework in which perception occurs. You do not see the same world differently; you see (or exist within) a DIFFERENT WORLD that follows different spatial rules. The 2D flattening, the dimensional folding, the conveyor belt sensation — these are not visual distortions but ontological disruptions. The nature of space itself is altered, and visual perception (to the extent it continues to function) reflects that altered space.

**Key distinction:** If psychedelics transform perception (same world, different appearance) and ketamine degrades perception (same world, less clarity), salvia REPLACES perception (different world entirely). The visual world is not enhanced or impaired — it is swapped out for a dimensional space that does not obey the rules of ordinary physics. This is the most radical perceptual disruption of any substance in this project.

### Dimension 3: Cognitive — THOUGHT FRAGMENTATION, LANGUAGE DISSOLUTION, CONCEPTUAL THINKING

Salvia's cognitive effects are among the most severe of any psychoactive substance. Language production — the ability to form words, sentences, or even concepts in words — is profoundly disrupted.

**Low dose:**
- Unusual thought patterns — thoughts feel "different" in quality, not necessarily in content
- Mild confusion — difficulty following conversational threads
- Slight dissociation between thought and language — thinking one thing, saying another, or being unable to articulate a thought
- Sense of being "slowed down" cognitively

**Moderate dose:**
- **Language interference** — severe difficulty forming words or sentences; speech becomes fragmented, repetitive, or impossible
- **"Thinking in concepts, not words"** — distinctive salvia phenomenon: cognitive processing continues but language is inaccessible; thoughts exist as pre-linguistic "packages" of meaning that cannot be translated into words
- **Thought fragmentation** — the linear flow of thought breaks apart; ideas exist as disconnected fragments with no logical connection
- **External control of thought** — thoughts feel imposed, forced, or controlled by an external agency (the "being pulled" sensation applies to cognition too)
- **Loss of categorical thinking** — the ability to categorize, sort, or organize information collapses; concepts that normally belong to different categories merge
- **Reality testing failure** — complete inability to distinguish the salvia reality from ordinary reality; during the experience, salvia reality IS reality
- **Amnesia for ordinary reality** — forgetting that ordinary reality exists, or that there is a "normal" state to return to
- **Compulsive thought loops** — the same thought or concept cycles endlessly; inability to break out of a cognitive pattern
- **"Am I real?"** — fundamental questioning of one's own existence becomes experiential, not philosophical

**Breakthrough dose:**
- **Complete language dissolution** — no words, no sentences, no linguistic thought whatsoever; language may seem like an absurd or impossible system
- **Conceptual collapse** — the building blocks of thought themselves dissolve; not just words but CONCEPTS become unavailable
- **Cognitive replacement** — if any cognition persists, it operates in a mode completely different from ordinary thought: non-linguistic, non-logical, non-sequential
- **Post-experience amnesia** — large portions of the experience may be unrecoverable upon return to ordinary consciousness
- **The "forgetting"** — a distinctive salvia phenomenon: during the experience, you may "forget" who you are, what reality is, what a human is, what ANYTHING is, as if all knowledge is stripped away
- **Inability to recognize** — objects, people, situations may be present visually but not recognized; you can see but not understand what you are seeing
- **The "reset"** — upon return, cognitive function resumes as if nothing happened; the experience is forgotten almost as quickly as it ends, creating a paradoxical amnesia for what may have been the most intense experience of one's life

**Key distinction:** Psychedelic cognition EXPANDS (more associations, more meaning, more connections). Ketamine cognition DETACHES (same thoughts, but they feel like they belong to someone else). Salvia cognition DISSOLVES — language fails, concepts fail, categories fail, and if anything remains it operates in a mode that cannot be translated into ordinary thought. The experience may be profound, but it resists integration precisely because the cognitive tools required for integration (language, concepts, narrative) are what salvia destroys. This is the primary reason salvia experiences are so difficult to describe and integrate afterward.

### Dimension 4: Emotional — DYSPHORIA, TERROR, CONFUSION, PARADOXICAL PROFUNDITY

Salvia's emotional profile is the most consistently negative of any substance in this project — and this is a pharmacological feature, not a bug. KOR activation directly activates the brain's endogenous dysphoria system (dynorphin pathway), making unpleasant emotion a structural consequence of the mechanism, not a random occurrence.

**Low dose:**
- Unease, apprehension, "something is wrong"
- Mild anxiety about bodily sensations and perceptual changes
- Bizarre emotional tone — not clearly positive or negative, but distinctly WRONG
- Confusion about what is happening and why

**Moderate dose:**
- **Dysphoria** — pervasive unpleasant emotional tone; everything feels wrong, off, disturbing
- **Terror** — the perceptual and cognitive disruption is experienced as threatening; fear of losing control, losing sanity, losing reality permanently
- **Confusion** — profound disorientation about what is real, who you are, what is happening; the confusion itself is distressing
- **Helplessness** — the experience is happening TO you, not BY you; there is no agency, no control, no ability to influence the experience
- **Paradoxical amusement** — despite (or because of) the intensity, some users report a bizarre detached amusement or sense of absurdity about the impossibility of what they are experiencing
- **Guilt / shame** — some users report inexplicable feelings of guilt, shame, or being "judged" by an external presence
- **Existential confusion** — not anxiety about dying (that is ketamine) but confusion about EXISTING — "What am I? What is this? Why is there something instead of nothing?"
- **NOT euphoric** — salvia is the only substance in this project that is reliably NOT euphoric at any dose level

**Breakthrough dose:**
- **Complete emotional overwhelm** — emotions, if present at all, are too intense and too alien to categorize as "fear" or "confusion" — they exist beyond the normal emotional spectrum
- **Annihilation anxiety** — the dissolution of self and reality produces terror that goes beyond fear of death into fear of NEVER HAVING EXISTED
- **OR: emotional void** — at complete breakthrough, emotion may simply cease; there is no feeling because there is no self to feel
- **The "test" or "judgment"** — some users experience the encounter with "the lady" as a test, lesson, or judgment — often confusing, sometimes terrifying, occasionally profound

**Post-experience emotional integration:**
- **Rapid emotional recovery** — because the experience is so brief (5–15 minutes), the acute emotional distress resolves quickly
- **Residual unease** — days to weeks of background anxiety, especially about the ontological implications of the experience ("Is reality real?")
- **Paradoxical profundity** — despite the predominantly negative emotional character during the experience, many users report that the experience was among the most profound of their lives; they would not repeat it, but they are changed by it
- **Difficulty integrating** — the experience resists narrative integration because the cognitive tools for creating narrative (language, sequential thought, conceptual frameworks) were disabled during the experience
- **Not therapeutic in the conventional sense** — unlike psilocybin or ketamine, salvia has not shown therapeutic potential; the dysphoric, aversive, and confusing character makes clinical application challenging

**Key distinction:** MDMA produces love and warmth. Psilocybin produces awe and wonder. Ketamine produces detachment or void. Salvia produces dysphoria, confusion, and terror. This is NOT a deficiency — it is a direct consequence of KOR activation, which is the brain's endogenous anti-reward and stress-response system. Salvia activates the neurochemical circuitry that produces suffering, then amplifies it to extraordinary intensity. The paradoxical profundity that many users report may arise from the confrontation with this most fundamental form of unpleasant experience.

### Dimension 5: Ego / Self — FORCED DISSOLUTION, BECOMING OBJECTS, IDENTITY FRAGMENTATION

Salvia dissolves the self not through expansion (psychedelics), separation (ketamine), or softening (MDMA), but through FORCED DISSOLUTION — the self is torn apart, folded, compressed, or annihilated by an apparent external force.

| Psychedelic ego dissolution | Ketamine ego dissolution | Salvia ego dissolution |
|---|---|---|
| Ego dissolves INTO the world (merging) | Ego dissolves AWAY FROM the world (separation) | Ego is DESTROYED by an external force |
| Boundaries between self and other dissolve | Boundaries between self and body dissolve | The very CONCEPT of self becomes incoherent |
| Volitional surrender is the path to dissolution | Dissolution happens progressively through detachment | Dissolution is FORCED — you have no choice |
| "I am everything" | "I am nothing" | "I am not" / "What is 'I'?" |
| Oceanic, expansive, cosmic | Void-like, empty, formless | Dimensional, mechanistic, alien |
| Often experienced as liberating | Often experienced as peaceful OR terrifying | Often experienced as confusing, terrifying, profound |

**Low dose:**
- Self feels "unusual" or "wrong" — not depersonalized in the ketamine sense, but ontologically disturbed
- Mild identity confusion — "Something about me is different"
- Sense of being observed or judged by an external presence

**Moderate dose:**
- **Forced ego dissolution** — the self does not gradually dissolve; it is pulled apart, compressed, or annihilated by an external force
- **Becoming an object** — identity transforms entirely; you are no longer a person but a book, a surface, a wheel, a dimension, a concept
- **Merging with environment** — the boundary between self and world collapses completely; you ARE the room, the furniture, the reality
- **Identity fragmentation** — the self does not dissolve into unity (psychedelics) or into nothingness (ketamine) but into FRAGMENTS — pieces of self that belong to different objects, dimensions, or realities
- **"Being turned into something"** — distinctive salvia experience: the transformation of identity from human to non-human; this is not metaphorical but experiential
- **Loss of autobiographical memory** — during the experience, you may not remember your name, your life, your relationships, or that you ARE a person
- **The "revelation of fiction"** — the experience of realizing (experientially, not intellectually) that your identity, your life, your reality are constructed — like turning a page and discovering you are a character in a story you did not know you were in
- **Compulsive identity cycling** — rapidly shifting between different identity states: self, object, nothing, other

**Breakthrough dose:**
- **Complete identity annihilation** — there is no self, no identity, no "I," no concept of personhood
- **Ontological shock** — the realization (experiential) that the self never existed in the way you thought it did
- **The "book" or "story" revelation** — the experience of reality as a narrative or construction that is being read, written, or revealed
- **The "lady" as identity authority** — in many reports, the entity encounter involves the entity somehow creating, controlling, or revealing the nature of the self
- **Post-experience identity destabilization** — upon return, the knowledge (or suspicion) that the self is a construct can persist for hours to days, producing existential confusion

**The entity encounter and ego:**
Salvia's ego dissolution is unique in that it is often experienced as INTERPERSONAL — the self is dissolved not by internal processes but by an EXTERNAL ENTITY. "The lady Salvia," "la pastora," "the shepherdess" — this feminine presence is one of the most consistent features of salvia phenomenology and is experienced as the agent of ego dissolution. She (it is almost always gendered feminine) may:
- Pull, push, or fold the self
- Reveal the constructed nature of reality/identity
- Judge, test, or teach
- Create or destroy the sense of self
- Exist as the "author" or "creator" of reality, with the self being her creation

This interpersonal quality of ego dissolution is unique to salvia — psychedelic ego death is self-to-cosmos merging, ketamine ego death is self-into-void separation, and salvia ego death is self-destroyed-by-other.

**Key distinction:** In the Altered States framework, salvia's ego dissolution is the most radical and the most alien. It is not transcendent (psychedelics), not peaceful (ketamine void), not softening (MDMA). It is FORCED, MECHANICAL, and EXTERNAL. The self is not dissolved through expansion or detachment but through a process experienced as happening TO you BY something else. This is why salvia experiences are so often described as terrifying — the self has no agency in its own dissolution.

### Dimension 6: Relational / Mystical — ENTITY ENCOUNTERS, PARADOXICAL PROFUNDITY, THE LADY

Salvia's relational/mystical dimension is the most distinctive and culturally significant of its phenomenological features. Entity encounters — particularly with a feminine entity referred to as "the lady Salvia," "la pastora," or "the shepherdess" — are among the most consistent and well-documented features of the salvia experience, with roots in centuries of Mazatec traditional use.

**Entity encounters — "The Lady Salvia":**
- The most distinctive feature of salvia's mystical dimension
- Almost universally described as feminine: "she," "her," "the lady," "the mother," "the shepherdess"
- Experienced not as a hallucination but as a REAL PRESENCE — as real or more real than ordinary reality
- Roles attributed to the entity:
  - **Guide** — leading or pulling the user through dimensional spaces
  - **Creator/Author** — revealed as the force that creates/maintains reality; the experience is of discovering that reality is her construction
  - **Judge** — examining, testing, or evaluating the user
  - **Teacher** — revealing hidden truths about the nature of reality, self, or existence
  - **Mechanical operator** — in some accounts, she is experienced as the mechanism by which reality operates, like a loom operator weaving the fabric of existence
  - **Indifferent** — in other accounts, she is not interactive but simply present, like a force of nature
- The entity encounter is NOT consensual — the user does not choose to meet "the lady"; she appears and acts upon the experience
- The entity is NOT necessarily benevolent — she may be experienced as neutral, confusing, hostile, or beyond moral categories

**Mystical/unity experiences:**
- **Less reliably mystical than psychedelics** — salvia does not consistently produce the "mystical experience" described by Pahnke, Stace, or Griffiths [Source 8]
- **Paradoxical profundity** — despite the predominantly dysphoric and confusing character, the experience is often retrospectively interpreted as profoundly meaningful. Users describe it as "the most intense experience of my life" while simultaneously describing it as "the worst experience of my life"
- **Unity states are RARE** — unlike psilocybin (where unity with cosmos is common) or 5-MeO-DMT (where unity is the defining feature), salvia rarely produces a sense of oneness with everything
- **Unity-with-entity** — when unity states occur, they are typically unity with THE ENTITY, not with the cosmos or nature
- **Ineffability** — salvia experiences are among the MOST difficult to describe of any psychoactive substance; the conceptual and linguistic disruption during the experience means that the content of the experience resists translation into language afterward

**The Mazatec context** [Source 4]:
- The Mazatec people have used Salvia divinorum for centuries in divinatory rituals
- "Ska María Pastora" — the Mazatec understood salvia as a tool for communicating with a spiritual entity (the shepherdess)
- The entity encounter is not a Western projection — it is central to the indigenous framework for understanding salvia
- The Mazatec consider salvia less powerful than psilocybin mushrooms, but still a legitimate tool for spiritual divination
- The feminine gendering of the entity is consistent across Western and indigenous accounts, suggesting it may be a phenomenological constant of KOR-mediated altered states

**Post-experience spiritual/mystical integration:**
- Rapid recovery (5–15 minutes) means the acute experience is very brief
- The experience often defies integration — it resists narrative construction, cannot be adequately described in language, and does not clearly map onto existing spiritual frameworks
- Some users report lasting changes in worldview: increased awareness of reality as constructed, decreased attachment to ego/identity, increased sense of the mysterious or unknown
- Other users report no lasting positive effects — the experience was unpleasant and confusing, and they have no desire to repeat it
- Salvia has NOT shown therapeutic potential comparable to psilocybin or ketamine; the dysphoric, confusing, and aversive character limits clinical applicability

**Key distinction:** DMT produces entity encounters in a context of cosmic awe and transport to alien dimensions — the entities are experienced as inhabitants of a hyperspace. Psilocybin produces mystical unity with the cosmos through the 5-HT2A pathway. Salvia produces entity encounters in a context of dysphoria, confusion, and forced reality dissolution through the KOR pathway — the entity is experienced as the AUTHOR or MECHANISM of reality itself. The encounters share the feature of perceived otherness, but their emotional tone, interpretive framework, and phenomenological texture are fundamentally different.

## 3. Dose Arc — The Fastest Altered State in the Project

Salvia has the most compressed temporal arc of any psychoactive substance: onset within 30–60 seconds, peak at 2–5 minutes, and complete return to baseline by 15–20 minutes. The entire experience from first effect to full recovery is often shorter than the onset time of psilocybin.

| Phase | Timing | Phenomenology |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-onset** | 0–30 seconds | Normal consciousness; sometimes anticipatory anxiety |
| **Onset** | 30–60 seconds | First effects: warmth, vibration, gravity shift, "something is starting"; body begins to feel wrong; perceptual stability begins to waver |
| **Acceleration** | 60 seconds – 2 minutes | Rapid intensification of ALL dimensions simultaneously; reality begins to shift, fold, or dissolve; body loses coherence; language starts to fail; the "pulling" sensation begins; thought fragmentation accelerates |
| **Peak (breakthrough)** | 2–5 minutes | Full reality dissolution; complete ego death; dimensional replacement; entity encounters possible; complete language failure; total disorientation; the most intense 2–5 minutes possible in a psychoactive experience |
| **Early descent** | 5–10 minutes | Rapid but disorienting return; reality reassembles but feels wrong, fragile, or provisional; confusion about what just happened; language returns but thoughts are fragmented; "Did that actually happen?" |
| **Late descent** | 10–15 minutes | Increasing coherence; motor function returns; ability to form sentences returns; emotional confusion resolves; residual physical sensations (sweating, ataxia, unusual bodily feelings) |
| **Afterglow** | 15–30 minutes | Near-baseline with residual confusion, mild perceptual alteration, and a powerful sense of "what just happened?" — the experience is already fading from memory |
| **Post-experience** | 30 min – hours/days | Residual existential confusion, difficulty describing the experience, paradoxical sense of profundity despite (or because of) the negative emotional character; the experience is already becoming a half-remembered dream |

**Route differences:**

| Route | Onset | Peak | Duration (total) | Intensity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Smoked (leaf)** | 20–60 seconds | 1–3 minutes | 5–15 minutes | Moderate-high | Traditional vaporization using torch lighter; dose varies with leaf potency |
| **Smoked (extract)** | 15–30 seconds | 1–2 minutes | 5–10 minutes | Very high to breakthrough | 5x–40x standardized extracts; most common form in recreational use |
| **Vaporized (pure salvinorin A)** | 30–60 seconds | 2–5 minutes | 10–20 minutes | Breakthrough | Pure compound; most controlled administration |
| **Sublingual quid (fresh leaf)** | 5–15 minutes | 20–40 minutes | 45–90 minutes | Light-moderate | Traditional Mazatec method; requires large quantities of fresh leaf; much gentler experience |
| **Oral (swallowed)** | Minimal | Negligible | Negligible | Negligible | Salvinorin A is destroyed by first-pass metabolism; oral bioavailability ~1–5% |

**Critical pharmacokinetic features** [Sources 2, 7]:
- Salvinorin A is rapidly metabolized to salvinorin B (inactive metabolite) — this metabolism is the primary reason for the extreme brevity of effects
- Half-life in humans is estimated at ~1 hour, but subjective effects end long before full elimination because the threshold concentration for effects is relatively high
- Poor oral bioavailability (1–5%) explains why traditional quid use requires large amounts of fresh leaf and why smoking produces dramatically more intense effects
- The rapid onset (seconds when smoked) and rapid offset (minutes) create a phenomenological experience with no parallel in pharmacology — it is the most temporally compressed intense altered state known

**The amnesia problem:**
- Salvia's brevity creates a paradox: it may produce the most intense experience of a person's life, but the experience is almost impossible to remember afterward
- The cognitive disruption during the experience (language dissolution, conceptual collapse) means that memories are not encoded in a format accessible to ordinary consciousness
- Users often report: "I know something profound happened, but I cannot remember what it was"
- This amnesia is one of the most distinctive features of salvia and a major barrier to integration, therapeutic application, and even accurate phenomenological reporting

## 4. Challenging Experiences

**The default is challenging:**
- Unlike other substances where challenging experiences are exceptional (psilocybin: ~30% at high doses; ketamine: variable), salvia experiences are CHALLENGING BY DEFAULT
- The dysphoric, confusing, terrifying character of the experience is not a "bad trip" — it is the EXPECTED response to KOR activation
- "Good" salvia experiences (where "good" means not terrifying) are the exception, not the rule

**Specific challenging phenomena:**
- **Forced dissolution panic** — the experience of the self being destroyed by an external force with no ability to resist, stop, or influence the process; this produces a form of panic that is qualitatively different from psychedelic anxiety because there is NO agency to surrender
- **Reality annihilation terror** — not fear of death (ketamine) but fear of never having existed, or of discovering that reality is fictional/constructed
- **The "can't come back" fear** — despite salvia's extreme brevity, during the experience there is no way to know that it will end; the intensity creates genuine fear of permanent alteration
- **Complete helplessness** — the experience of having zero control, zero agency, zero ability to influence what is happening; this is distinct from psychedelic helplessness (where surrender is possible) because there is no self to surrender
- **Confusion rather than insight** — salvia produces confusion, not insight; the experience does not resolve into understanding (unlike many psychedelic experiences) but ends with bewilderment
- **Physical danger** — the loss of motor coordination, spatial orientation, and situational awareness creates genuine risk of injury (falling, walking into objects, drowning if near water); a sitter is essential
- **The entity as threatening** — "the lady Salvia" is not always benevolent; she may be experienced as judgmental, hostile, indifferent, or overwhelmingly powerful

**Management of challenging experiences:**
- **IMPOSSIBLE to "talk down" during the experience** — because language comprehension is destroyed at moderate-to-high doses, verbal reassurance cannot be processed
- **Physical safety is the primary intervention** — remove dangerous objects, prevent falls, ensure the person cannot wander
- **Quiet, dark environment** — reduce sensory input; external stimuli during salvia are experienced as overwhelming and confusing
- **Wait** — this is the only intervention that works; the experience WILL end within 15 minutes regardless
- **Post-experience grounding** — once language returns, provide reassurance, orientation (who, where, when), and physical comfort (water, blanket, warmth)
- **KEY DIFFERENCE from psychedelic challenges:** psychedelic challenges are managed by guiding surrender, acceptance, and trust. Salvia challenges cannot be managed at all during the experience — there is no one home to guide. The only management strategy is PHYSICAL SAFETY and WAITING. Afterward, the primary need is orientation and reassurance that the experience is over and reality is stable.
- **Do NOT attempt to "use" the experience** — unlike psychedelic integration (which benefits from processing the content of the experience), salvia integration focuses on recovering from the disorientation and accepting that the experience resists verbal processing

**Salvia's distinctive quality:** "The Doorway" — salvia is the most phenomenologically radical substance in the Altered States project, and the most difficult to integrate. It produces the fastest, most intense, most reality-destroying altered state known, through a mechanism (KOR agonism) that is unique in pharmacology. The experience is characterized by forced ego dissolution through an apparent external agency, dimensional shifting that replaces rather than modifies reality, complete language and conceptual failure, and a consistently dysphoric emotional valence. Its very brevity (5–15 minutes) is paradoxical — the most intense experience of a lifetime, compressed into less time than a coffee break, and often impossible to remember afterward. The entity encounters ("the lady Salvia") connect contemporary Western experiences to centuries of Mazatec divinatory practice, making salvia the only substance in this project with a documented indigenous phenomenological framework that aligns with modern self-report.

Salvia occupies a category of its own in psychopharmacology — not a psychedelic, not a dissociative, not an entactogen, but a **kappa opioid hallucinogen**, the only member of a class that may be defined by its unique mechanism. Its value in the Altered States project is as the outlier — the substance that demonstrates how far altered states can deviate from the serotonergic and glutamatergic frameworks, and how radical consciousness alteration can be when it arises from the brain's own endogenous suffering system (dynorphin/KOR).
