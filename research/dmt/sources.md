## 1. Strassman & Qualls (1994) — Foundational IV DMT Dose-Response

**Title:** Dose-response study of N,N-dimethyltryptamine in humans. I. Neuroendocrine, autonomic, and cardiovascular effects / II. Subjective effects and preliminary results of a new rating scale
**Authors:** Rick J. Strassman, Clifford R. Qualls, E.H. Uhlenhuth, Robert Kellner
**Journal:** *Archives of General Psychiatry*, 51(2), 85–108
**Year:** 1994
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7700711/
**Tags:** [clinical] [dose-response] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** First FDA-approved clinical DMT study in 20+ years. N=12, IV doses: 0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.4 mg/kg. Established the HRS (Hallucinogen Rating Scale). At 0.4 mg/kg: complete visual replacement, entity encounters (~50% of sessions), "launched/catapulted" somatic sensation. Onset within 30-60 seconds IV, peak at 2-5 min, resolution by 20-30 min. Documented the alien/technological quality of entity environments.

## 2. Strassman (2001) — DMT: The Spirit Molecule

**Title:** DMT: The Spirit Molecule: A Doctor's Revolutionary Research into the Biology of Near-Death and Mystical Experiences
**Author:** Rick Strassman
**Publisher:** Park Street Press
**Year:** 2001
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology] [first-person]

**Annotation:** Expanded qualitative reports from 60 volunteers across ~400 IV DMT sessions at University of New Mexico (1990-1995). Entity typologies: clowns, elves, insects, reptilians — often in technological/futuristic environments. Subjects described being probed, tested, shown things, receiving information telepathically. Many spontaneously compared experiences to alien abduction. The "more real than real" quality was a consistent finding.

## 3. Timmermann et al. (2019) — Neural Correlates (EEG)

**Title:** Neural correlates of the DMT experience assessed with multimodal neuroimaging
**Authors:** Christopher Timmermann, Leor Roseman, Luke Williams, David Erritzoe, et al., Robin L. Carhart-Harris
**Journal:** *Scientific Reports*, 9, 16324
**Year:** 2019
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-019-51974-4
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** N=13, 20mg IV DMT, simultaneous EEG. DMT produced the highest neural signal diversity (entropy) of ANY psychedelic measured. Marked decreases in alpha/beta power; increases in theta (immersive visionary state). Brain activity resembled REM sleep — yet experiences were rated "more real than real." The neural paradox: dreaming-like brain, hyperreal experience. Changes within seconds of injection.

## 4. Timmermann et al. (2023) — EEG-fMRI Combined Imaging

**Title:** Human brain effects of DMT assessed via EEG-fMRI
**Authors:** Christopher Timmermann, et al., Robin L. Carhart-Harris
**Journal:** *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS)*, 120(13), e2218949120
**Year:** 2023
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2218949120
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** First combined EEG-fMRI of DMT. N=20, 20mg IV. DMT produces simultaneous DECREASE in within-network connectivity (DMN dissolves) and INCREASE in between-network connectivity (hyperconnectivity). More extreme than LSD or psilocybin. The brain's reality-modeling systems operate at full power but freed from normal constraints — explaining "being transported somewhere else."

## 5. Timmermann et al. (2018) — DMT Models Near-Death Experience

**Title:** DMT Models the Near-Death Experience
**Authors:** Christopher Timmermann, Leor Roseman, Luke Williams, et al., Robin L. Carhart-Harris
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Psychology*, 9, 1424
**Year:** 2018
**URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.01424/full
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** IV DMT in 13 volunteers compared with NDE accounts using Greyson Scale. All 13 scored above NDE threshold despite being in no medical danger. Overlapping features: unearthly realm, mystical being encounters, feelings of dying, tunnel experience, "more real than real." DMT is the only psychedelic that reliably mimics near-death experiences on validated instruments.

## 6. Davis, Clifton & Griffiths (2020) — Entity Encounter Survey

**Title:** Survey of entity encounter experiences occasioned by inhaled N,N-dimethyltryptamine: Phenomenology, interpretation, and enduring effects
**Authors:** Alan K. Davis, John M. Clifton, Eric G. Weaver, Ethan S. Hurley, Roland R. Griffiths
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychopharmacology*, 34(9), 1008–1020
**Year:** 2020
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1177/0269881120916143
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [first-person]

**Annotation:** Largest entity encounter survey ever (N=2,561 smoked/vaporized DMT users). Entity types: beings (60%), guides (43%), spirits (39%), aliens (39%). Perceived as conscious/intelligent (96%), benevolent (78%), sacred (70%). 69% received a "message." 75% rated it among top 5 most meaningful experiences of their life. 72% believed entities were independently real. Atheist identification dropped from 28% to 10% post-experience.

## 7. Michael, Luke & Robinson (2021) — Breakthrough Phenomenology

**Title:** An Encounter With the Other: A Thematic and Content Analysis of DMT Experiences From a Naturalistic Field Study
**Authors:** Pascal Michael, David Luke, Oliver Robinson
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Psychology*, 12, 720717
**Year:** 2021
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.720717
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [first-person]

**Annotation:** N=36, detailed qualitative analysis of smoked DMT. Identified the consistent "breakthrough" sequence: (1) visual distortion → (2) geometric tunnel/chrysanthemum → (3) "breaking through" membrane → (4) immersion in hyperreal alien space → (5) entity contact → (6) return. The space: vast, luminous, geometric, technological, crystalline. Entities: autonomous, intelligent, intentional, emotionally warm, non-human. "Hyperreal" quality confirmed.

## 8. Riba et al. (2001/2003) — Ayahuasca Pharmacology

**Title:** Subjective effects and tolerability of the South American psychoactive beverage Ayahuasca in healthy volunteers / Human pharmacology of ayahuasca
**Authors:** Jordi Riba, Antoni Rodríguez-Fornells, et al., Manel J. Barbanoj
**Journal:** *Psychopharmacology* / *JPET*
**Year:** 2001/2003
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s002130000606
**Tags:** [clinical] [dose-response] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** First controlled ayahuasca studies. N=18, oral doses 0.6-1.0 mg DMT/kg. Onset: 30-60 min, peak: 1.5-2h, duration: 4-6h. Ayahuasca visuals more narrative, autobiographical, culturally-rich than smoked DMT (landscapes, ancestors, serpents, mythological themes). Eyes-open perceptions distorted but maintained (vs smoked DMT's complete replacement). Nausea/purging in ~50%. Ego dissolution gradual, not instantaneous. The same molecule, radically different phenomenology by route.

## 9. Shanon (2002) — The Antipodes of the Mind

**Title:** The Antipodes of the Mind: Charting the Phenomenology of the Ayahuasca Experience
**Author:** Benny Shanon
**Publisher:** Oxford University Press
**Year:** 2002
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [first-person]

**Annotation:** The definitive ayahuasca phenomenology mapping. 130+ personal sessions + ~180 interviews. Catalogued recurring content across cultures: serpents (most common), jaguars, birds, palaces/cities, divine beings, eyes. Staged progression: somatic → geometric → figurative → narrative → mystical/noetic. Ayahuasca is "the teacher plant" — pedagogical, narrative, ceremonial. Cross-cultural content consistency (serpents, jaguars, palaces appear regardless of expectation).

---

## 10. Frecska, Bokor & Winkelman (2016) — Theoretical Framework

**Title:** The Therapeutic Potentials of Ayahuasca: Possible Effects against Various Diseases of Civilization
**Authors:** Ede Frecska, Pál Bokor, Michael Winkelman
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Pharmacology*, 7, 35
**Year:** 2016
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** Theoretical framework positioning DMT as endogenous neuromodulator (naturally occurring in human CSF, blood, lung tissue). Sigma-1 receptor agonism (unique to DMT among classic psychedelics) implicated in cellular stress response, neuroprotection, consciousness modulation. Links DMT entity encounters to broader theory of consciousness and non-local information processing. May explain NDE parallels.
