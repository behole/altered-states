## 1. Carhart-Harris et al. (2016) — Landmark Neuroimaging Study

**Title:** Neural correlates of the LSD experience revealed by multimodal neuroimaging
**Authors:** Robin L. Carhart-Harris, Suresh Muthukumaraswamy, Leor Roseman, Mendel Kaelen, Wouter Droog, Kevin Murphy, et al.
**Journal:** *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS)*, 113(17), 4853–4858
**Year:** 2016
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1518377113
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** The first modern brain imaging study of LSD in over 40 years. N=20, 75 μg IV LSD. Key findings: DMN disintegration correlates with ego dissolution (similar to psilocybin), V1 "liberation" explains eyes-closed hallucinations, "entropic brain" effect — LSD pushes brain activity toward higher entropy. Critical distinction: LSD produced more widespread connectivity changes and greater thalamic connectivity shifts than psilocybin, possibly explaining the "analytical/electric" quality.

## 2. Kraehenmann, Preller & Vollenweider (2015) — Amygdala Fear Response

**Title:** Acute effects of LSD on amygdala activity during processing of fearful stimuli in healthy subjects
**Authors:** Rainer Kraehenmann, Katrin H. Preller, Milan Scheidegger, Thomas Pokorny, Oliver G. Bosch, Erich Seifritz, Franz X. Vollenweider
**Journal:** *Biological Psychiatry*, 78(3), 186–192
**Year:** 2015
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.biopsych.2014.12.010
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [clinical] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** N=20, 100 μg oral LSD, placebo-controlled. LSD significantly reduced left amygdala reactivity to fearful stimuli. Reduced fear reactivity correlated with 5D-ASC Oceanic Boundlessness scores — mystical experience is neurally linked to altered threat processing. Dominant emotional shift was toward positive valence despite anxiogenic reputation. 5D-ASC showed predominantly positive ego-transcendence (high OBN, moderate AED).

## 3. Holze & Liechti (2021) — Definitive Dose-Response Study

**Title:** Acute dose-dependent effects of lysergic acid diethylamide in a double-blind placebo-controlled study in healthy subjects
**Authors:** Friederike Holze, Patrick Vizeli, Laura Ley, Felix Müller, Patrick Dolder, Milos Stober, et al., Matthias E. Liechti
**Journal:** *Neuropsychopharmacology*, 46, 537–544
**Year:** 2021
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41386-020-00883-6
**Tags:** [clinical] [dose-response] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** The gold-standard dose-response study. N=16, five sessions: placebo, 25 μg, 50 μg, 100 μg, 200 μg. All 5D-ASC dimensions scaled dose-dependently. Critical finding: the 100→200 μg step is a qualitative shift — ego dissolution and mystical experience have steep dose-response curves. 25 μg = minimal; 50 μg = threshold; 100 μg = robust altered state; 200 μg = full mystical experience with profound ego dissolution. Somatic: pupil dilation at all doses, jaw tension ≥100 μg, temperature ≥100 μg.

## 4. Holze, Ley & Liechti (2022) — Head-to-Head LSD vs Psilocybin

**Title:** Acute effects of LSD and psilocybin in a double-blind placebo-controlled cross-over study in healthy subjects
**Authors:** Friederike Holze, Laura Ley, Felix Müller, Anna M. Becker, Isabelle Straumann, Patrick Vizeli, et al., Matthias E. Liechti
**Journal:** *Neuropsychopharmacology*, 47(6), 1180–1187
**Year:** 2022
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41386-022-01297-2
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** The single most important paper for distinguishing LSD from psilocybin. Same participants received LSD 100 μg, psilocybin 20 mg, and placebo. At equipotent doses, subjective effect profiles were MORE SIMILAR THAN DIFFERENT on 5D-ASC. Duration was the most robust differentiator (12h vs 6h). LSD showed slightly more complex imagery. This nuances the "electric vs organic" narrative — the qualitative distinction may be amplified by duration, set/setting, and dopaminergic overlay rather than fundamentally different phenomenology.

## 5. Kaelen et al. (2015/2017) — LSD and Music

**Title:** LSD enhances the emotional response to music
**Authors:** Mendel Kaelen, Frederick S. Barrett, Leor Roseman, et al., Robin L. Carhart-Harris
**Journal:** *Psychopharmacology*, 232(19), 3607–3614 (2015); 234(19), 3067–3076 (2017)
**Year:** 2015/2017
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s00213-015-4014-y
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [clinical]

**Annotation:** N=20, 75 μg IV LSD. Music profoundly modulates the LSD experience — enhanced wonder, transcendence, power, tenderness (GEMS scales). Music-evoked emotion predicted insightfulness. Eyes-closed imagery was directed by music content. LSD's extended duration allows music to scaffold extended narrative arcs of meaning-making. Synesthetic quality: music produces vivid visual imagery under LSD. Follow-up (2018) showed music-evoked emotion predicts therapeutic outcomes.

## 6. Dolder & Liechti (2016) — Empathy and Sociality

**Title:** LSD acutely impairs fear recognition and enhances emotional empathy and sociality
**Authors:** Patrick C. Dolder, Yasmin Schmid, Felix Müller, Stefan Borgwardt, Matthias E. Liechti
**Journal:** *Neuropsychopharmacology*, 41(11), 2638–2646
**Year:** 2016
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/npp.2016.82
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** N=24, 100 μg LSD. Key dissociation: LSD impairs cognitive empathy (intellectually identifying emotions) while enhancing emotional empathy (felt resonance). Reduced fear/anger recognition in faces — social world appears less threatening. Enhanced prosociality, openness, trust. LSD's empathogenic quality is distinct from MDMA (which enhances both types) and more outward-facing than psilocybin's inward introspection. Dopaminergic component may contribute.

## 7. Nour, Evans, Nutt & Carhart-Harris (2016) — Ego Dissolution Inventory

**Title:** Ego-dissolution and psychedelics: Validation of the Ego-Dissolution Inventory (EDI)
**Authors:** Matthew M. Nour, Lisa Evans, David Nutt, Robin L. Carhart-Harris
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*, 10, 269
**Year:** 2016
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2016.00269
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [clinical]

**Annotation:** EDI validation across N=691 (retrospective survey including LSD) plus prospective data (N=20, 75 μg IV). Two poles of ego dissolution: oceanic (blissful surrender) vs anxious (frightening loss of self). Ego dissolution is the gateway to mystical experience — without it, experience stays perceptual/cognitive. LSD ego dissolution tends to be more gradual onset and longer duration than psilocybin, allowing more integration during the experience.

## 8. Kettner et al. (2021) — Phenomenological Comparison Across Psychedelics

**Title:** The varieties of the psychedelic experience: A preliminary phenomenological study
**Authors:** Hannes Kettner, Fernanda E. Rosas, Christopher Timmermann, Leor Kärtner, Robin L. Carhart-Harris, Leor Roseman
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Psychiatry*, 12, 697625
**Year:** 2021
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyt.2021.697625
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [first-person]

**Annotation:** Large-scale survey (N>900) comparing phenomenological profiles across LSD, psilocybin, DMT, and ayahuasca. LSD rated higher for geometric/abstract visual patterns, synesthesia, and thought acceleration. Psilocybin rated higher for entity encounters and emotional catharsis. LSD = "cosmic/revelatory"; psilocybin = "earthy/intimate." Critically: LSD challenging experiences characterized by thought loops, paranoia, and being "trapped" for 8+ hours; psilocybin challenges = grief, fear, emotional overwhelm in shorter burst. Strongest empirical support for electric/analytical vs organic/emotional distinction.

## 9. Passie et al. (2008) — Comprehensive Pharmacology Review

**Title:** The pharmacology of lysergic acid diethylamide: A review
**Authors:** Torsten Passie, John H. Halpern, Dirk O. Stichtenoth, Hinderk M. Emrich, Annelie Hintzen
**Journal:** *CNS Neuroscience & Therapeutics*, 14(4), 295–314
**Year:** 2008
**URL:** https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6494066/
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** The definitive pharmacology review. Establishes LSD's broader receptor profile vs psilocybin: 5-HT2A + D1/D2 dopamine + 5-HT2C/1A/6/7 + adrenergic. D2 agonism is the pharmacological "smoking gun" for the electric/analytical quality — contributes wakefulness, analytical cognition, grandiosity, stimulant energy. Phase model: (1) Somatic 0-1h, (2) Perceptual 1-4h, (3) Psychodynamic 3-8h, (4) Resolution 8-12h. Complete cross-tolerance with psilocybin confirms shared 5-HT2A mechanism.

## 10. Dolder, Schmid & Liechti (2015) — Pharmacokinetics

**Title:** Pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics of lysergic acid diethylamide in healthy subjects
**Authors:** Patrick C. Dolder, Yasmin Schmid, Markus Haschke, Katharina M. Rentsch, Matthias E. Liechti
**Journal:** *Clinical Pharmacokinetics*, 55(10), 1219–1230
**Year:** 2015
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s40262-016-0385-9
**Tags:** [clinical] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Hard pharmacokinetic numbers. Onset: 30-45 min oral. Tmax (peak plasma): 1.5h. Peak subjective effects: 2-3h. Plateau: hours 2-5. Descent begins: hours 5-6. Total duration: 8-12h (mean ~11h at 200 μg, ~8.5h at 100 μg). Plasma half-life: ~2.6h. Key: subjective effects last much longer than plasma levels predict because LSD's receptor "lid" closes over the molecule (Wacker et al. 2017), trapping it at 5-HT2A. LSD is ~100x more potent by weight than psilocybin.
