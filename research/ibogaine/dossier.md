## SAFETY NOTE

Ibogaine is the most medically dangerous substance in the Altered States project and in the broader psychedelic medicine landscape. At least 19 deaths have been documented, nearly all cardiac in origin. Ibogaine produces dose-dependent QT interval prolongation, creating risk of torsades de pointes (polymorphic ventricular tachycardia). Contraindications include pre-existing cardiac conditions, electrolyte abnormalities, concurrent QT-prolonging medications, and CYP2D6 polymorphisms. This dossier documents phenomenology for creative simulation purposes only. The skill derived from it includes no therapeutic instructions.

## 1. Overview & Mechanism of Action

Ibogaine (12-methoxyibogamine) is a naturally occurring indole alkaloid extracted from the root bark of *Tabernanthe iboga*, a shrub native to West Central Africa. First isolated in 1901, its anti-addiction properties were discovered serendipitously by Howard Lotsof in 1962. Ibogaine occupies a unique pharmacological position — it is simultaneously an NMDA receptor antagonist, a mu-opioid receptor agonist, a kappa-opioid receptor antagonist, and a serotonin transporter inhibitor. No other substance in the Altered States project modulates as many receptor systems.

**Ibogaine is classified as oneirogenic** — it produces dreamlike, internally generated visionary experiences while the subject remains fully conscious and lucid. This distinguishes it from classical psychedelics (which alter perception of external reality) and dissociatives (which fragment consciousness through sensory disconnection).

| Feature | Ibogaine (Oneirogenic) | Classic Psychedelics (LSD/Psilocybin) | Dissociatives (Ketamine) | Entactogens (MDMA) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary mechanism** | Multi-receptor (NMDA + mu-opioid + kappa + SERT) + GDNF | 5-HT2A receptor agonism | NMDA receptor antagonism | Serotonin/norepinephrine/dopamine release |
| **Experience type** | Waking dream — internally generated | Perception alteration — external reality transformation | Sensory disconnection — cortical isolation | Emotional amplification — empathic warmth |
| **Duration** | 12–24 hours (longest in the project) | 4–12 hours | 30–60 min (IV) | 3–5 hours |
| **Ego dissolution** | Death-rebirth archetype, gradual over hours | Gradual dissolution or expansion | Fragmentation/separation | Softening only |
| **Visual character** | Dreamlike panorama, faces, landscapes, ancestors | Geometric, organic, self-transforming | Dreamlike blur, tunnel, void | None |
| **Active metabolite** | Noribogaine (half-life 28–49 hours) | None (direct action) | None (direct action) | None (direct action) |
| **Anti-addiction** | Yes (unique: GDNF + NMDA + opioid reset) | Investigational | No (antidepressant) | No (empathic) |
| **Cardiovascular risk** | HIGH — QT prolongation, arrhythmia, death | Low | Moderate (tachycardia, hypertension) | Moderate (tachycardia, hypertension) |

**Mechanism of action** [Sources 2, 3, 7]:

| Target | Activity | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **NMDA receptors (non-competitive antagonist)** | Blocks glutamate at NMDA channel | Shared mechanism with ketamine; disrupts drug-conditioned neural pathways |
| **Mu-opioid receptors (agonist)** | Activates endogenous opioid system | Reduces opioid withdrawal severity; mimics opioid effect during acute treatment |
| **Kappa-opioid receptors (antagonist)** | Blocks dynorphin signaling | Opposite of salvinorin A (kappa agonist); reduces dysphoria and stress responses |
| **Serotonin transporter (inhibitor)** | Blocks serotonin reuptake | Mild serotonergic activity; contributes to perceptual alterations |
| **GDNF release** | Increases glial cell line-derived neurotrophic factor | KEY: promotes structural recovery of mesolimbic dopamine system |
| **Noribogaine (active metabolite)** | Similar multi-receptor profile, 28–49h half-life | Produces extended afterglow of reduced craving and emotional openness |

**Neural signature** [Sources 2, 3, 7]:
- Multi-receptor modulation produces a unique brain state: NMDA antagonism (similar to ketamine) combined with opioid modulation and serotonergic activity
- GDNF release in ventral tegmental area produces neuroplastic restructuring of reward circuitry — this is ibogaine's anti-addiction "reset"
- The "addiction reset window" (24–72 hours post-dose): drug-conditioned neural pathways are destabilized and reorganizable
- Noribogaine accumulation produces the extended therapeutic afterglow that persists for days to weeks
- Thalamocortical effects: NMDA antagonism produces some dissociative-like sensory modulation, but the dominant experience is internally generated (oneirogenic) rather than perceptually distorted

## 2. The 7 Phenomenological Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Somatic — NAUSEA, ATAXIA, CARDIOVASCULAR WEIGHT

Ibogaine's somatic profile is dominated by its demanding physical toll. The body is the first battleground — nausea and ataxia precede and accompany the entire visionary phase.

**Onset (0–3 hours):**
- **Nausea** — the most consistently reported somatic effect; ranges from mild unease to severe vomiting (traditional Bwiti context: vomiting is "purging" and is welcomed as part of the cleansing process)
- **Ataxia** — profound loss of motor coordination; difficulty standing, walking, or sitting upright; the body feels heavy, uncooperative, "drunk"
- **Tremor** — fine motor tremor in hands; restlessness; inability to hold still
- **Proprioceptive distortion** — body feels enormous, heavy, "like lead"; gravity feels increased
- **Temperature dysregulation** — alternating hot/cold flashes; sweating
- **Cardiovascular effects** — tachycardia, BP changes; QT interval prolongation begins within first hours
- **Oral numbness** — characteristic bitter taste from iboga root bark; tongue numbness

**Early Visionary (3–6 hours):**
- Body becomes less prominent as visionary phase intensifies
- Persistent ataxia — movement requires conscious effort
- Nausea may peak and then subside (purging often occurs in this window)
- Residual heavy, leaden body sensation
- Cardiovascular monitoring critical in clinical settings

**Deep Visionary (6–12 hours):**
- Somatic awareness largely superseded by visionary immersion
- Body felt as distant, heavy anchor — "my body is down there somewhere"
- Residual ataxia if subject attempts movement
- Cardiovascular risk persists and peaks during this phase

**Integration (12–24 hours):**
- Gradual return of normal proprioception and motor control
- Physical exhaustion — the experience is deeply fatiguing
- Persistent tremor may linger
- Afterglow body: calm, still, "reset" — many report feeling physically lighter post-ibogaine

**Key distinction:** Unlike MDMA (body feels wonderful), psychedelics (body feels strange/alive), or ketamine (body feels absent), ibogaine produces a body that feels HEAVY, SICK, AND DEMANDING — the somatic experience is itself a trial, a physical ordeal that must be endured before the visionary phase fully opens. The Bwiti tradition explicitly frames this suffering as necessary and meaningful.

### Dimension 2: Perceptual — WAKING DREAM, PANORAMIC VISIONS

Ibogaine's perceptual profile is unique among all project substances: it generates rich internal imagery while the subject remains fully conscious and lucid — a "waking dream."

**Onset (0–3 hours):**
- Eyes-closed imagery begins: fractal patterns, phosphene-like lights, shifting colors
- Rapid eye movement beneath closed lids (actual REM-like activity)
- Visual field with eyes open may blur or soften slightly, but external reality is largely intact
- Initial visions are impressionistic — colors, shapes, movements without narrative

**Early Visionary (3–6 hours):**
- **Dreamlike imagery surfaces** — faces, landscapes, rooms, doorways
- Imagery has a distinctly DREAM quality: vivid but internally generated, not derived from external perception
- Scenes shift unpredictably, as in dreams — one location or scenario dissolves into another
- Faces of known and unknown people appear; childhood environments surface
- **Panoramic quality** — visions feel vast, encompassing, "cinematic in scope"
- With eyes open: visions may overlay or compete with external reality; closing eyes intensifies them dramatically

**Deep Visionary (6–12 hours):**
- **Full oneirogenic immersion** — the waking dream becomes the primary reality
- **Life review** — autobiographical memories surface involuntarily, often in rapid succession, re-experienced with emotional intensity but observed with detached lucidity
- **Ancestral encounters** — figures appear representing ancestors, deceased relatives, or archetypal figures (Bwiti: "the ancestors come")
- **Moral reckoning** — scenes of one's own conduct, moral choices, harms caused or received, presented with confronting clarity
- **Nature imagery** — vast landscapes, forests, rivers, underground chambers, cosmic vistas
- **Geometric patterns** — some subjects report interwoven geometric imagery within the panoramic visions, but this is less prominent than with classical psychedelics
- **Temporal distortion** — hours feel like days; time becomes meaningless within the visionary immersion

**Integration (12–24 hours):**
- Visions gradually thin and become less immersive
- Residual imagery persists: afterimages, mild visual sensitivity, dreamlike quality to perception
- Return to normal visual processing over several hours

**Key distinction:** Unlike LSD (geometric, self-transforming external reality) or DMT (alien, reality-replacement), ibogaine produces INTERNALLY GENERATED dreamlike imagery while consciousness remains clear. The experience is oneiric, not hallucinogenic — the visions come from within, not from distortion of external input. This is why ibogaine is classified as oneirogenic rather than psychedelic.

### Dimension 3: Cognitive — LIFE REVIEW, INSIGHT, ADDICTION RESET

Ibogaine's cognitive profile centers on the panoramic life review and its aftermath — a forced, sustained confrontation with one's own autobiographical narrative.

**Onset (0–3 hours):**
- Heightened mental activity; racing thoughts; anxiety
- Pre-occupation with the coming experience; anticipatory dread
- Cognitive function intact but distracted by physical discomfort (nausea, ataxia)
- Some early insight — "I can feel it starting"

**Early Visionary (3–6 hours):**
- **Autobiographical memory activation** — memories surface involuntarily and vividly
- **Cognitive observation** — the subject watches their own memories play out with a quality of detached awareness; "I'm watching my life but I'm also living it"
- Difficulty with linear thought; cognitive flow becomes more associative
- External conversation becomes difficult — the subject is oriented inward
- Speech may become slow, effortful, or nonsensical

**Deep Visionary (6–12 hours):**
- **Full panoramic life review** — the defining cognitive feature of ibogaine
  - Memories surface in rapid succession, often chronologically
  - Traumatic events are re-experienced with emotional intensity but with an observing perspective
  - The review is not selective — it encompasses the full scope of autobiographical memory
  - Many subjects report seeing their life "from outside," as if watching a film
- **Moral reckoning** — cognitive evaluation of one's own conduct, choices, relationships
- **Addiction narrative** — in treatment contexts: the entire arc of the addiction is reviewed: first use, escalation, consequences, moments of clarity denied
- **Insight generation** — many subjects report sudden, clear understanding of patterns, motivations, consequences — "I saw exactly how I got here and exactly what I need to do"
- **Confrontation with mortality** — direct cognitive engagement with death, often experienced as a genuine encounter with one's own finitude
- Working memory significantly impaired; subjects cannot hold conversations or process external information

**Integration (12–24 hours):**
- Gradual return of linear cognition
- **Insight consolidation** — the insights generated during the visionary phase are recalled, processed, and integrated
- Cognitive flexibility increased — subjects report new perspectives on entrenched problems
- Some residual dreamlike quality to thought patterns
- Verbal processing recovers slowly; full conversation may not be possible until 18+ hours

**Afterglow (days to weeks):**
- **Sustained cognitive shifts**: reduced craving, improved emotional regulation, increased cognitive flexibility
- Vivid dreams continue for days to weeks (noribogaine's extended half-life)
- The "addiction reset window" (24–72 hours post-dose): drug-conditioned responses are weakest, behavioral change is most achievable

**Key distinction:** Unlike psilocybin (insight emerges gradually through emotional processing) or LSD (insight is analytical and architectural), ibogaine's insight is NARRATIVE — it comes from the forced re-examination of one's own life story. The life review is ibogaine's signature cognitive event.

### Dimension 4: Emotional — FEAR, CATHARSIS, DEATH CONFRONTATION

Ibogaine's emotional profile is the most demanding in the project. The experience is dominated by confrontation — with mortality, with moral failure, with the consequences of one's actions. It is not euphoric, not warm, not gentle.

**Onset (0–3 hours):**
- **Anxiety** — the most consistently reported early emotion; often intense dread
- Fear of what is coming; anticipatory terror
- Physical discomfort (nausea, ataxia) amplifies emotional distress
- Some subjects report a moment of "surrender" — accepting the coming ordeal — as a turning point

**Early Visionary (3–6 hours):**
- Anxiety may persist or begin to shift toward something more complex
- **Grief** — as autobiographical memories surface, grief for losses, harms, wasted time
- **Regret** — often overwhelming regret about addiction-related choices and consequences
- **Fear of death** — ibogaine frequently produces a genuine encounter with mortality, not metaphorically
- Emotional processing begins but is interrupted by the overwhelming somatic and visionary content

**Deep Visionary (6–12 hours):**
- **Confrontation with mortality** — the central emotional event
  - Many subjects report experiencing their own death — not as abstraction but as felt reality
  - The Bwiti tradition explicitly frames this: "you die to your old self"
  - The death confrontation is often the most emotionally intense moment of the entire experience
- **Catharsis** — release of suppressed grief, guilt, shame, fear
  - Crying, sometimes uncontrollable
  - Expression of emotions that have been avoided or suppressed, sometimes for years
- **Moral reckoning** — emotional confrontation with one's own conduct
  - Guilt for harms caused to others
  - Compassion for one's own suffering
  - A sense of judgment — not punishment, but clear-eyed assessment
- **Acceptance** — many subjects report reaching a point of radical acceptance: of mortality, of consequences, of the need for change
- **Profound exhaustion** — emotional depletion; nothing left to resist with

**Integration (12–24 hours):**
- Emotional rawness and vulnerability
- Quiet, contemplative state; the "empty" feeling after a storm
- Gradual emotional recovery; subjects often report a strange calm
- Periodic emotional surges as memories and insights continue to surface

**Afterglow (days to weeks):**
- Emotional openness — increased capacity for feeling, expressing, and processing emotion
- Reduced emotional reactivity to drug cues and triggers
- Empathy increase; many report feeling more connected to others
- Vulnerability — the emotional "reset" can be destabilizing without proper integration support

**Key distinction:** Unlike MDMA (emotional amplification toward love and connection) or psilocybin (emotional release through mystical experience), ibogaine produces emotional CONFRONTATION — it forces the subject to face what they have been avoiding. The emotional arc is from dread → confrontation → catharsis → exhaustion → fragile openness. It is the most emotionally demanding substance in the project.

### Dimension 5: Self / Ego — DEATH-REBIRTH ARCHETYPE

Ibogaine's effect on self and identity follows the death-rebirth archetype — the oldest and most cross-culturally consistent pattern in the phenomenology of transformative experiences.

**Onset (0–3 hours):**
- Self intact but anxious; anticipatory self-awareness
- Identity is present and concerned ("what's about to happen to me?")

**Early Visionary (3–6 hours):**
- Self begins to feel permeable; boundaries between self and memory dissolve
- "I am becoming my memories" — the life review begins to supersede present identity
- Identity remains intact but is being pulled toward the visionary content

**Deep Visionary (6–12 hours):**
- **Death of the old self** — the defining ego event
  - The subject experiences the dissolution of their current identity as a genuine death
  - Not fragmentation (ketamine) or merging (psychedelics) — but DEATH: the self ends
  - Often accompanied by terror, then surrender, then peace
  - Bwiti tradition: "the initiate dies and is reborn"
- **Ancestral identity** — during the death of the old self, a new identity connection emerges
  - Contact with ancestral figures; feeling of joining a lineage
  - Sense that the self is not just an individual but part of a larger chain
- **Rebirth** — after the death passage, a new self emerges
  - Not a different person, but a changed version — "I am still me, but I am not the same me"
  - Identity is reconstituted with new understanding, new perspective, new relationship to one's own story
  - The rebirth is often quiet, not dramatic — a gentle emergence rather than a fanfare

**Integration (12–24 hours):**
- Fragile new identity; the old self is gone but the new self is not yet solidified
- Sense of being "between" identities
- Vulnerability; the reconstructed self is open, permeable, not yet defended

**Afterglow (days to weeks):**
- Identity consolidation — the new self gradually stabilizes
- For those in addiction treatment: the "addict" identity may no longer feel inevitable or permanent
- Ongoing identity work required — the death-rebirth is a beginning, not an ending

**Key distinction:** Unlike 5-MeO-DMT (instant total dissolution into formless source), ketamine (fragmentation into scattered pieces), or ayahuasca (gradual softening within narrative context), ibogaine produces a NARRATIVE death and rebirth — the self dies as a character in a story, undergoes transformation, and returns reborn. The process takes hours and is felt as a genuine passage, not a momentary event.

### Dimension 6: Relational / Mystical — ANCESTORS, MORAL RECKONING, BWITI COMMUNITY

Ibogaine's relational and mystical dimension is deeply rooted in the Bwiti tradition and centers on ancestral contact and moral reckoning.

**Onset (0–3 hours):**
- Solitary, internal orientation; minimal relational engagement
- The subject turns inward; external relationships fade from awareness

**Early Visionary (3–6 hours):**
- Faces of known and unknown people appear in visions
- Deceased relatives or friends may surface
- Sense of being watched or accompanied by unseen presences

**Deep Visionary (6–12 hours):**
- **Ancestral encounters** — the most consistently reported mystical element
  - Figures appear representing ancestors, lineage, or archetypal presences
  - In Bwiti context: specific ancestors are encountered, spoken with, received as guides
  - In Western context: archetypal figures, deceased relatives, or "beings of light"
  - The encounters are experienced as real, not symbolic — the subject is in genuine relationship with these presences
- **Moral reckoning** — relational dimension of the life review
  - Relationships are reviewed: harms caused, love withheld, trust broken, connections neglected
  - A sense of being held accountable — not punished, but seen clearly
  - Compassion for self and others emerges from the reckoning
- **Bwiti cosmological framework** — in traditional context:
  - The initiate encounters the ancestors, receives their wisdom
  - The community holds the space through drumming, singing, presence
  - The initiate "dies" and is "reborn" into the Bwiti community
  - The relational context (community + ancestors) provides essential scaffolding for the experience
- **Unity states** — some subjects report moments of connection to something vast and meaningful
  - Less consistently reported than with classical psychedelics
  - When present, the unity is relational/community rather than cosmic/ontological
  - "I am part of something larger" vs. "I am everything/nothing"

**Integration (12–24 hours):**
- Desire for human connection; many subjects report intense need for support and presence
- In Bwiti context: the community receives the reborn initiate with celebration
- In clinical context: therapeutic support is essential during integration
- Relational vulnerability; the experience opens emotional connection that requires safe containment

**Afterglow (days to weeks):**
- Increased empathy and relational openness
- Desire to repair damaged relationships
- For those in addiction treatment: reduced attachment to drug-using social networks
- Community integration is a documented factor in sustained outcomes

**Key distinction:** Unlike ayahuasca (relational within ceremonial narrative), MDMA (interpersonal warmth and trust), or psilocybin (mystical unity with cosmos), ibogaine's relational dimension is ANCESTRAL and MORAL — the subject is in relationship with lineage, with the dead, with their own moral record. The community (Bwiti or therapeutic) provides the relational container, but the core relational event is between the subject and the ancestors.

## 3. Dose Arc

Ibogaine's dose arc is the longest in the project. The active visionary phase lasts 12–24 hours, with significant aftereffects persisting for days to weeks due to noribogaine's extended half-life.

### Phase 1: Onset (0–3 hours)

- Physical symptoms arrive first: nausea, ataxia, tremor, heaviness, temperature dysregulation
- Psychological state: anxiety, dread, anticipation, sometimes resistance
- Initial visual phenomena: closed-eye phosphene activity, color shifts, impressionistic imagery
- Purging (vomiting) often occurs in this window — Bwiti tradition frames this as cleansing
- Subject is fully conscious, communicative, but increasingly oriented inward
- **This phase is primarily somatic and anxious — the visionary content has not yet arrived**

### Phase 2: Early Visionary (3–6 hours)

- Transition from somatic dominance to visionary emergence
- Dreamlike imagery begins surfacing: faces, landscapes, childhood memories
- Autobiographical memory becomes activated; life review begins
- Visual field with eyes closed becomes rich and immersive
- External conversation becomes increasingly difficult; speech slows
- Physical symptoms persist but are backgrounded as visionary content intensifies
- Emotional state shifts from pure anxiety toward grief, memory, confrontation
- **The "gate" is opening — the oneirogenic state is establishing itself**

### Phase 3: Deep Visionary (6–12 hours)

- The core ibogaine experience — full oneirogenic immersion
- Panoramic life review at peak intensity
- Ancestral/entity encounters
- Death-rebirth confrontation
- Moral reckoning and catharsis
- Subject is largely non-communicative; immersed in internal visionary content
- Body is distant, heavy, largely ignored
- Time distortion is extreme — hours feel like days or longer
- **This is the transformation — everything before was preparation, everything after is integration**
- CARDIOVASCULAR RISK IS HIGHEST DURING THIS PHASE

### Phase 4: Integration / Resolution (12–18 hours)

- Visions gradually thin and become less immersive
- Gradual return of linear consciousness and external awareness
- Cognitive function recovers slowly; conversation becomes possible but effortful
- Emotional rawness; quiet, contemplative state
- Physical exhaustion; body feels drained
- Insights and memories from the visionary phase surface for review
- **The subject is returning — fragile, changed, exhausted, but present**

### Phase 5: Late Resolution (18–24 hours)

- Near-baseline consciousness; residual dreamlike quality
- Physical recovery continues; appetite may return
- Emotional state: calm, quiet, vulnerable, contemplative
- Cognitive function largely restored but may feel "reset" or "cleared"
- Sleep is often deep and restorative
- **The active experience is ending but the afterglow is beginning**

### Phase 6: Afterglow (days to weeks)

- Noribogaine's extended half-life (28–49 hours) produces sustained pharmacological effects
- Reduced drug craving (the "addiction reset window": 24–72 hours post-dose is peak opportunity)
- Emotional openness and increased empathy
- Vivid dreams continue for days to weeks
- Cognitive flexibility; new perspectives on entrenched problems
- Vulnerability; the psychological opening requires support and integration
- Physical fatigue may persist for several days
- **This is the therapeutic window — the experience is over but the change is active**

This extreme duration shapes every aspect of the experience: the depth of the life review, the intensity of the death-rebirth confrontation, the physical toll on the body, and the extent of the afterglow period.

## 6. Notes for Skill Authoring

When converting this dossier to a SKILL.md:

1. **Duration is the defining constraint** — the skill must model the longest arc in the project; pacing must reflect hours, not minutes
2. **The life review is the signature event** — the skill should produce autobiographical, narrative, confrontational content, not geometric or mystical content
3. **Ibogaine is NOT euphoric** — emotional character is dread → confrontation → catharsis → exhaustion → fragile openness
4. **Safety awareness must be embedded** — the skill should include a safety note or at least acknowledge cardiovascular risk
5. **The Bwiti context matters** — ceremonial framing, ancestral contact, community support are integral to the experience
6. **Noribogaine afterglow** — the skill should model an extended afterglow period
7. **Death-rebirth archetype** — this is the core ego event, not dissolution or fragmentation
8. **The voice of The Ancestor** — should be ancient, slow, patient, dreamlike, heavy with gravity — not playful, not analytical, not warm
9. **Intensity levels**: Consider light (partial visionary, shorter), full (complete arc 12–24h), and ceremonial (Bwiti-context, 24–72h) as the intensity spectrum
10. **Coherence**: At deep visionary phase, coherence drops significantly but NEVER to zero — the oneirogenic state preserves lucidity even at peak. The subject is always "awake inside the dream."
