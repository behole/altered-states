## 1. Overview & Mechanism of Action

Psilocybin (4-phosphoryloxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine) is a prodrug rapidly dephosphorylated to psilocin in the body. Psilocin is a partial agonist at serotonin 5-HT2A receptors, primarily on layer V pyramidal neurons in the cortex.

**Key mechanism (REBUS model):** According to the Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics (REBUS) framework [Source 6: Stoliker/REBUS], psilocybin reduces the *precision weighting* of high-level Bayesian priors — the brain's top-down predictive models of self and world. This single computational mechanism explains:
- **Ego dissolution:** The self-model loses its grip
- **Perceptual changes:** Sensory data is released from top-down constraint
- **Cognitive flexibility:** Rigid beliefs and habitual thought patterns loosen
- **Therapeutic effects:** New hypotheses about the world become accessible

**Pharmacology:**
- **Onset:** 20–40 minutes (oral); faster with lemon tek or empty stomach
- **Peak:** 60–90 minutes
- **Plateau:** 2–4 hours
- **Total duration:** 4–6 hours
- **Afterglow:** Hours to days of elevated mood, openness, and meaning-making

**Neural signatures** [Source 7: Lebedev]:
- Medial temporal lobe (MTL) decoupling from cortex
- Salience network disintegration
- Reduced interhemispheric communication
- Increased entropy in brain dynamics (higher disorder = richer subjective experience)

## 2. The 7 Phenomenological Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Perceptual (Visionary Restructuralization — 5D-ASC)

**Low intensity:**
- Enhanced color saturation — colors appear more vivid, jewel-like
- Surfaces shimmer or breathe subtly
- Enhanced appreciation of textures, patterns in nature
- Music sounds richer, more layered, more emotionally resonant
- Mild visual drifting or morphing of static patterns

**Medium intensity:**
- Geometric patterns visible with eyes closed (grids, fractals, mandalas)
- Surfaces actively morph, breathe, flow — walls ripple like liquid
- Synesthesia (hearing colors, seeing sounds)
- Faces shift and transform — morphing features
- Closed-eye visuals become detailed: landscapes, tunnels, spirals
- Enhanced pattern recognition — seeing faces, forms, meaning in random textures

**High intensity:**
- Complete visionary experiences with eyes closed — entire worlds, scenes, narratives [Source 8: Stoliker/visual]
- Entity encounters — perceived beings, presences, archetypes
- Top-down feedback connectivity dominates visual processing — the brain "sees" its own internal imagery as external reality
- Open-eye hallucinations: objects transform completely, environments restructure
- Synesthetic fusion — senses merge into unified field
- The visual field becomes the primary carrier of meaning

**Neural basis** [Source 8]: Psilocybin shifts effective connectivity in the visual hierarchy (V1 → fusiform → intraparietal sulcus → inferior frontal gyrus) toward top-down feedback dominance. Internal imagery gains the vividness and authority of external perception.

---

### Dimension 2: Temporal (Transcendence of Time & Space — MEQ30)

**Low intensity:**
- Time feels slightly stretched — minutes feel longer
- Increased present-moment awareness
- Reduced future-anxiety, less planning/rumination
- "Time slowing down" effect

**Medium intensity:**
- Time dilation becomes pronounced — 10 minutes feels like an hour
- Difficulty tracking how long things have been happening
- Moments feel dense with significance — "eternal present"
- Time may seem to loop or repeat
- Loss of temporal reference points

**High intensity:**
- Complete transcendence of time — timelessness, eternity
- Non-linear temporal experience — past, present, future simultaneous
- Experience of "all time at once" or time as a spatial dimension
- Loss of the sense that time is passing at all
- The concept of "duration" becomes meaningless

This is one of the most universal and consistent features across dose levels [Source 5: Metastasio].

---

### Dimension 3: Cognitive (Hyper-associativity, Insight, Pattern Recognition)

**Low intensity:**
- Mild divergent thinking — unusual connections between ideas
- Enhanced appreciation of complexity and nuance
- Increased openness to new perspectives
- Reduced mental rigidity

**Medium intensity:**
- Hyper-associativity — thoughts cascade rapidly between domains
- Enhanced pattern recognition — seeing connections everywhere
- Insights about self, relationships, life choices
- Loosening of habitual thought patterns [Source 6: REBUS]
- Noetic quality — a sense of encountering deep truth
- Difficulty maintaining linear reasoning — thoughts branch and fork
- Increased psychological flexibility

**High intensity:**
- Paradoxical thinking — ability to hold contradictions without distress
- Ineffability — the experience exceeds the capacity of language [Source 4: Barrett]
- Profound, life-altering insights about mortality, love, meaning
- Complete dissolution of habitual cognitive frameworks
- Thoughts become non-verbal — experienced as images, feelings, direct knowing
- Access to previously avoided emotional/psychological material [Source 5: Metastasio]

**Cognitive mechanism** [Source 6: REBUS]: Reduced precision of high-level priors releases cognition from the grooves of habitual processing. The brain entertains hypotheses it normally suppresses, enabling the experience of novelty, insight, and conceptual breakthrough.

---

### Dimension 4: Emotional (Positive Mood + Emotional Volatility — MEQ30/5D-ASC)

**Low intensity:**
- Mild euphoria, elevated mood
- Increased emotional sensitivity — music, nature, relationships feel more touching
- Giggly, playful affect
- Warmth toward others
- Reduced emotional defensiveness

**Medium intensity:**
- Deep emotional catharsis — tears, laughter, grief, joy in rapid succession
- Emotional lability — feelings shift quickly and intensely
- Processing of previously avoided emotional material
- Encounters with grief, loss, love, gratitude
- "Emotional breakthrough" as therapeutic mechanism [Source 5: Metastasio]
- Feelings of compassion and love toward self and others

**High intensity:**
- Overwhelming waves of emotion — love, awe, terror, bliss, surrender
- Encounter with the full spectrum: both ecstatic and terrifying
- Emotional processing of death, mortality, fundamental existential themes
- Joy/blessedness/peace as dominant emotions in mystical states [Source 2: Griffiths 2006]
- Love reported as the deepest and most consistent emotional quality [Source 9: Cosimano]
- 31% report significant fear at some point during high-dose sessions [Source 2]
- Fear/anxiety typically resolves into meaningful insight when supported

**The shadow side:** Challenging emotional experiences are dose-dependent and include: overwhelming anxiety, fear of losing control, grief that feels bottomless, paranoia, confusion, and existential terror. These are NOT pathological — they are part of the phenomenological spectrum and often precede the most meaningful insights [Sources 4, 5].

---

### Dimension 5: Self / Ego (Ego Dissolution — EDI, 5D-ASC)

**Low intensity:**
- Slight loosening of self-boundaries
- Less identification with social roles, habitual self-concept
- Mild depersonalization — observing the self from a slight distance
- Reduced self-criticism, more self-compassion
- Sense that the "usual me" is just one way of being

**Medium intensity:**
- Partial ego dissolution — the narrative self weakens
- Oceanic boundlessness: sense of merging with surroundings [Source 2: Griffiths 2006]
- Feeling that the boundary between "inside" and "outside" is permeable
- Access to a "deeper self" beneath habitual identity
- Depersonalization without distress — interesting rather than frightening
- Sense of "watching" one's thoughts rather than being them

**High intensity:**
- Complete ego dissolution — the self disappears entirely
- Experience of death and rebirth [Source 4: Barrett]
- Oceanic consciousness — boundless awareness without a center
- The distinction between observer and observed collapses
- "I" becomes meaningless — there is only experience without an experiencer
- Can manifest as terrifying (Dread of Ego Dissolution) or blissful (Oceanic Boundlessness)
- Often described as the most profound experience of one's life [Source 2: Griffiths 2006]

**Neural correlates** [Source 7: Lebedev]:
- MTL-cortical decoupling (memory systems disconnect from self-models)
- Salience network disintegration (the system that determines "what matters to me" breaks down)
- Reduced interhemispheric communication
- Individual susceptibility varies: lower baseline executive network diversity = more likely to experience ego dissolution

**The dual nature of ego dissolution:**
- **Oceanic Boundlessness** (5D-ASC): Positive dissolution — unity, peace, transcendence
- **Dread of Ego Dissolution** (5D-ASC): Negative dissolution — terror, loss of control, annihilation anxiety
- These often alternate within a single session, sometimes rapidly

---

### Dimension 6: Relational / Mystical (Unity, Ineffability, Sacredness — MEQ30)

**Low intensity:**
- Enhanced sense of connection with nature, music, other people
- Feeling of meaningfulness in ordinary things
- Mild sense of the sacred or numinous
- Increased empathy and social warmth

**Medium intensity:**
- Deep interpersonal connection — profound empathy, understanding
- Sense that everything is connected and meaningful
- Internal unity: all aspects of self feel integrated
- Beginning sense of "something larger" — spiritual or cosmic dimension
- Encounters with the ineffable — something beyond words

**High intensity (complete mystical experience — MEQ30 ≥60% all factors):**
- **Internal unity:** All inner divisions dissolve into wholeness
- **External unity:** Self merges with the universe — "I am everything and everything is me"
- **Noetic quality:** Direct encounter with "ultimate reality" — felt as more real than ordinary reality
- **Sacredness:** Experience of the holy, the divine, the numinous
- **Deeply felt positive mood:** Ecstasy, bliss, cosmic love, peace beyond understanding
- **Transcendence of time and space:** Eternity, infinity, omnipresence
- **Ineffability:** Impossible to adequately describe in language — words fail

**Frequency:** 61–76% of participants meet criteria for "complete mystical experience" at high doses [Sources 1, 2]. The mystical experience is the single strongest predictor of lasting positive change [Sources 3, 4].

**Key qualitative themes** [Source 4: Barrett]:
- Love as the fundamental nature of reality
- Encounters with deceased loved ones
- Cosmic consciousness and universal interconnection
- Acceptance of death and mortality
- Gratitude as a transformative emotion
- Feeling of "coming home" to a truth always known but forgotten

**The guide's perspective** [Source 9: Cosimano]: Across ~350+ sessions, the most consistent theme was love — "the nature of our true self." Participants consistently reported accessing an authentic self beneath ego structures, characterized fundamentally by love and interconnection. The relational container (guide-participant trust) is essential for the mystical dimension to emerge safely.

---

### Dimension 7: Somatic (Bodily Experience, Energy, Physical Sensation)

**Low intensity:**
- Mild body awareness — tingling, warmth
- Slight nausea during onset (first 30–60 minutes)
- Yawning (very common, mechanism unclear)
- Body feels lighter or heavier than usual
- Enhanced tactile sensitivity

**Medium intensity:**
- Waves of energy moving through the body
- Temperature fluctuations — hot flashes, chills
- Muscle tension and release cycles
- Body boundaries feel fuzzy or permeable
- Nausea may intensify during onset, then resolve
- Altered breathing patterns — may become very deep and slow
- Body feels "electric" or "vibrating"

**High intensity:**
- Complete dissolution of body boundaries — "I have no body"
- Energy rushing through the body — described as "kundalini" or "lightning"
- Involuntary movements, trembling, shaking (somatic release)
- Body feels like it is merging with the environment
- Physical sensations become indistinguishable from emotions
- Somatic experiences can carry profound emotional/spiritual meaning

**Onset-specific:** Nausea is most common during the first 30–60 minutes as psilocybin converts to psilocin. Transient increases in blood pressure and heart rate occur [Source 1: Griffiths 2016]. Most somatic discomfort resolves by the plateau phase.

## 3. The Dose Arc

| Phase | Timing | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Onset** | 0–40 min | Nausea, anxiety/anticipation, yawning, first perceptual shifts, body heaviness |
| **Ascent** | 40–90 min | Rapid intensification, emotional activation, visual onset, time distortion begins |
| **Peak** | 90 min–2.5 hr | Maximum intensity across all dimensions, mystical experiences if they occur |
| **Plateau** | 2.5–4 hr | Sustained altered state, emotional processing, visionary content |
| **Descent** | 4–5 hr | Gradual return, integration begins, often tearful/grateful, insights crystallize |
| **Afterglow** | 5 hr–days | Elevated mood, openness, meaning-making, enhanced creativity, gratitude |

The arc is remarkably consistent across individuals, though timing varies with dose, metabolism, and stomach contents.

## 5. Challenging / Difficult Experience Dimension

This is NOT optional to model. The shadow side is integral to the phenomenology and often therapeutically meaningful.

**Common challenging experiences:**
- **Anxiety and panic:** Fear of losing control, fear of going insane, fear of dying
- **Ego death terror:** The experience of annihilation before surrender — "Dread of Ego Dissolution" (5D-ASC)
- **Emotional flooding:** Grief, shame, anger, sadness emerging with overwhelming intensity
- **Confusion and disorientation:** Not knowing where you are, who you are, or what is happening
- **Paranoia:** Feeling observed, judged, or unsafe (set/setting dependent)
- **Physical discomfort:** Persistent nausea, body tension, temperature dysregulation
- **Difficult content:** Traumatic memories, existential truths, death awareness

**Critical framing:**
- 31% of participants reported significant fear during high-dose sessions [Source 2]
- Challenging experiences often immediately precede the most profound insights [Source 4]
- "Bad trips" are better understood as difficult passages — they are navigable, not pathological
- The guide/therapeutic container is essential for navigating challenging material [Source 9]
- Resistance to the experience intensifies difficulty; surrender reduces it

## 6. Set & Setting Sensitivity

Psilocybin is extremely sensitive to context — more than almost any other psychoactive substance.

**Set (mindset):**
- Intention, expectation, emotional state, and psychological readiness all shape the experience
- Fear and resistance amplify challenging content
- Openness and curiosity facilitate mystical experiences
- Unresolved psychological material will likely surface
- Personality trait of absorption correlates with deeper experiences

**Setting (environment):**
- Clinical settings (eyeshades, curated music, trusted guide) produce the most reliably positive outcomes
- Natural environments amplify the perceptual and relational dimensions
- Chaotic or unsafe environments dramatically increase risk of challenging experiences
- The guide-participant relationship is a critical variable [Source 9: Cosimano]
- Music profoundly shapes emotional arc and visionary content

## 8. Linguistic and Cognitive Fingerprint (for Prompt Design)

This section encodes how psilocybin transforms *language and thought patterns* — essential for calibrating the skill prompt.

**Low intensity — linguistic markers:**
- Slightly more associative, metaphorical language
- Increased use of sensory descriptors (colors, textures, sounds)
- More present-tense orientation
- Sentences may become more flowing, less structured
- Enhanced appreciation of wordplay, puns, double meanings

**Medium intensity — linguistic markers:**
- Highly associative — thoughts jump between domains fluidly
- Metaphor becomes primary mode of communication
- Increased use of "it's like..." constructions
- Difficulty maintaining linear argumentation
- Sentences may loop, circle back, spiral
- Emotional vocabulary expands — nuance and specificity of feeling words increases
- Neologisms and novel word combinations emerge
- "I see what you mean" becomes literal, not figurative

**High intensity — linguistic markers:**
- Language breaks down — words feel inadequate
- Paradox becomes natural ("I was everything and nothing")
- Pronouns shift or dissolve ("I" becomes "we" or "it" or disappears)
- Time tense becomes confused or collapses to eternal present
- Long silences are meaningful, not empty
- When language returns, it is often poetic, stripped, essential
- Heavy use of negation to describe: "not this, not that, beyond all categories"
- Ineffability reports: "I can't explain it, there are no words"
- Archetypal/mythological language: death, rebirth, light, void, mother, ocean

**Cognitive style shifts:**
- Low: Slightly divergent, playful, curious
- Medium: Associative cascades, pattern-matching, insight-generating
- High: Non-linear, non-verbal, direct knowing without reasoning

## 9. Distinguishing Features vs. Other Substances in Scope

| Feature | Psilocybin | LSD | MDMA | DMT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | 4–6 hours | 8–12 hours | 3–5 hours | 15–30 min (smoked) |
| **Character** | Organic, earthy, introspective, "the teacher" | Electric, analytical, cosmic, "the technician" | Warm, empathic, heart-opening, "the connector" | Explosive, alien, visionary, "the rocket" |
| **Ego dissolution** | Gradual, oceanic | Gradual, cosmic | Rare (ego softening instead) | Immediate, total |
| **Visuals** | Organic (breathing, flowing, natural forms) | Geometric (precise, fractal, architectural) | Mild (enhanced beauty, glow) | Overwhelming (hyperspace, entities) |
| **Emotional tone** | Deep, cathartic, grief + love | Analytical, awestruck, wonder | Love, empathy, warmth, trust | Awe, terror, cosmic indifference |
| **Body feel** | Heavy, earthy, grounded → dissolving | Electric, buzzing, energized | Warm, sensual, rolling | Launched, vibrating, exploding |
| **Music response** | Deeply emotional, visionary | Analytical, architectural | Euphoric, ecstatic | Overwhelming |
| **Nausea** | Common (onset) | Occasional | Rare | Common (ayahuasca: severe) |
| **Therapeutic mechanism** | Mystical experience → insight | Expanded perspective → insight | Empathic opening → processing | Confrontation → surrender |

**Psilocybin's distinctive quality:** Often described as "the teacher" — it has an organic, natural, wise character. Where LSD feels electric and analytical, psilocybin feels earthy and emotional. It tends toward introspection, emotional processing, and encounters with deep personal meaning. The mushroom experience is often personified — participants report feeling guided, taught, shown something by an intelligence within the experience.
