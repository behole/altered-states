## 1. Riba et al. (2001/2003) — Ayahuasca Pharmacology

**Title:** Subjective effects and tolerability of the South American psychoactive beverage Ayahuasca in healthy volunteers / Human pharmacology of ayahuasca
**Authors:** Jordi Riba, Antoni Rodríguez-Fornells, et al., Manel J. Barbanoj
**Journal:** *Psychopharmacology* / *JPET*
**Year:** 2001/2003
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s002130000606
**Tags:** [clinical] [dose-response] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** First controlled ayahuasca studies. N=18, oral doses 0.6-1.0 mg DMT/kg. Onset: 30-60 min, peak: 1.5-2h, duration: 4-6h. Ayahuasca visuals more narrative, autobiographical, culturally-rich than smoked DMT (landscapes, ancestors, serpents, mythological themes). Eyes-open perceptions distorted but maintained (vs smoked DMT's complete replacement). Nausea/purging in ~50%. Ego dissolution gradual, not instantaneous. The same molecule, radically different phenomenology by route.

## 2. Shanon (2002) — The Antipodes of the Mind

**Title:** The Antipodes of the Mind: Charting the Phenomenology of the Ayahuasca Experience
**Author:** Benny Shanon
**Publisher:** Oxford University Press
**Year:** 2002
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [first-person]

**Annotation:** The definitive ayahuasca phenomenology mapping. 130+ personal sessions + ~180 interviews. Catalogued recurring content across cultures: serpents (most common), jaguars, birds, palaces/cities, divine beings, eyes. Staged progression: somatic → geometric → figurative → narrative → mystical/noetic. Ayahuasca is "the teacher plant" — pedagogical, narrative, ceremonial. Cross-cultural content consistency (serpents, jaguars, palaces appear regardless of expectation).

## 3. Frecska, Bokor & Winkelman (2016) — Theoretical Framework

**Title:** The Therapeutic Potentials of Ayahuasca: Possible Effects against Various Diseases of Civilization
**Authors:** Ede Frecska, Pál Bokor, Michael Winkelman
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Pharmacology*, 7, 35
**Year:** 2016
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** Theoretical framework positioning DMT as endogenous neuromodulator. Sigma-1 receptor agonism implicated in cellular stress response, neuroprotection, consciousness modulation. Relevant to ayahuasca as extended DMT exposure with MAOI potentiation. Links entity encounters to broader theory of consciousness.

## 5. Palhano-Fontes et al. (2019) — Rapid Antidepressant Effects (RCT)

**Title:** Rapid antidepressant effects of the psychedelic ayahuasca in treatment-resistant depression: a randomized placebo-controlled trial
**Authors:** Fernanda Palhano-Fontes, Draulio B. de Araujo, et al.
**Journal:** *Psychological Medicine*, 49(4), 655-665
**Year:** 2019
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29903051/
**Tags:** [clinical] [RCT] [antidepressant] [fMRI] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** First randomized placebo-controlled trial of ayahuasca for treatment-resistant depression. N=29, single dose. Significant rapid reduction in depression scores (HAM-D, MADRS) vs placebo. fMRI showed increased blood flow in left nucleus accumbens, right insula, and left subgenual area. Phenomenological data supports the "transcendental circle" pattern (vulnerability → fear/confrontation → purging → expansive peace). Key evidence that ayahuasca's antidepressant effect is mediated by altered emotional processing and brain regions involved in self-awareness and interoception.

## 6. Sanches et al. (2016) — Antidepressant Effects (SPECT)

**Title:** Antidepressant Effects of a Single Dose of Ayahuasca in Patients With Recurrent Depression: A SPECT Study
**Authors:** Rafael F. Sanches, Ligia R. Macedo, et al.
**Journal:** *Journal of Clinical Psychopharmacology*, 36(1), 77-81
**Year:** 2016
**URL:** https://repositorio.ufrn.br/items/8cb1963b-b2e9-42fa-b296-4ab096a6db31
**Tags:** [clinical] [SPECT] [antidepressant] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** SPECT study of cerebral blood flow in 17 patients with recurrent depression after single ayahuasca dose (2.2 mL/kg). Depression scores (HAM-D, MADRS) significantly reduced at 40min, 1d, 7d, 14d, and 21d post-dose. Increased blood perfusion in left nucleus accumbens, right insula, left subgenual area — regions involved in emotional regulation and reward. Nausea was the primary side effect. Confirms that ayahuasca's rapid antidepressant effect correlates with modulation of brain networks implicated in mood and emotional processing.

## 7. Hamill et al. (2019) — Comprehensive Review

**Title:** Ayahuasca: Psychological and Physiologic Effects, Pharmacology and Potential Uses in Addiction and Mental Illness
**Authors:** Jonathan F. Hamill, Jerry C. Lange, et al.
**Journal:** *Current Neuropharmacology*, 17(2), 108-128
**Year:** 2019
**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6343205/
**Tags:** [review] [pharmacology] [phenomenology] [safety]

**Annotation:** Comprehensive review of ayahuasca's psychological, physiological, and therapeutic profile. Documents the "transcendental circle" phenomenological pattern: vulnerability → confusion/paranoia → purging (peak terror) → abrupt shift to expansive peace/ecstasy/timelessness. Dose-dependent increases across all HRS subscales (somaesthesia, affect, volition, cognition, perception). Purging mechanism: serotonergic stimulation of vagus nerve (vomiting) and intestinal 5-HT (diarrhea). Visual effects include synesthesia (especially auditory→visual), geometric patterns, visions of animals, people, landscapes. Long-term ritual users show higher Self-Transcendence, Reward Dependence; lower Harm Avoidance. No evidence of psychological maladjustment, cognitive impairment, or addiction pattern. Hemodynamic effects less hazardous than alcohol, cocaine, marijuana, or MDMA.

## 8. Bouso et al. (2012) — Longitudinal Personality Study

**Title:** Personality, Psychopathology, Life Attitudes and Neuropsychological Performance among Ritual Users of Ayahuasca: A Longitudinal Study
**Authors:** José Carlos Bouso, Diana R. Gonzales, et al.
**Journal:** *PLoS ONE*, 7(8), e42421
**Year:** 2012
**URL:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0042421
**Tags:** [longitudinal] [personality] [psychopathology] [neuropsychology]

**Annotation:** Largest longitudinal study of regular ayahuasca users. N=127 users (15+ years use, 2x/month minimum) vs 115 matched religious controls. Users scored: higher Self-Transcendence, Reward Dependence; lower Harm Avoidance; lower on ALL psychopathology measures; better on Stroop, Wisconsin Card Sorting, Letter-Number Sequencing; higher on Spiritual Orientation, Purpose in Life, Psychosocial Well-Being. Differences maintained at 1-year follow-up. Users had lower Self-Directedness (interpreted as greater community relevance over individual). Santo Daime context: experiences interpreted as contact with God, spirits, entities from religious doctrine. Cross-cultural consistency in motivations and outcomes.
