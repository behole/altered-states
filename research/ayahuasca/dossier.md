## 1. Overview & Mechanism of Action

Ayahuasca is oral DMT (from Psychotria viridis) combined with beta-carboline MAO inhibitors (from Banisteriopsis caapi) that prevent first-pass metabolism of DMT in the gut.

**Same molecule as smoked DMT. Completely different experience.**

The MAOI component (harmine, harmaline, tetrahydroharmine from B. caapi) is not merely an enabler — it contributes its own psychoactive effects: sedation, tremor, and potentially its own visionary properties. The "ayahuasca experience" is the combination, not just oral DMT.

**Mechanism of action:**

| Target | Activity | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **5-HT2A** | Primary agonist (DMT) | Drives visual hallucinations, same as psilocybin/LSD |
| **MAO-A inhibition** | Beta-carbolines (B. caapi) | Prevents DMT metabolism, enables oral activity |
| **Sigma-1 receptor** | DMT agonist | Cellular stress response, may modulate entity encounters |
| **Extended exposure** | 4-6 hours vs 10-20 min | Slow rise, multiple peaks, narrative unfolding possible |

**The critical difference from smoked DMT:**
- Smoked: seconds to onset, complete reality replacement, 10-20 min, "the rocket"
- Ayahuasca: 30-60 min onset, reality augmented not replaced, 4-6 hours, "the teacher plant"
- The slow onset and extended duration allow narrative, autobiographical, and pedagogical content to develop — impossible in the 12-minute smoked window

| Feature | Smoked DMT | Ayahuasca |
|---|---|---|
| **Onset** | 5-60 seconds | 30-60 minutes |
| **Duration** | 10-20 minutes | 4-6 hours |
| **Visual character** | Complete replacement, alien, technological | Narrative, autobiographical, mythological, organic |
| **Entity character** | Machine elves, insects, aliens | Serpents, jaguars, ancestors, spirits, "Mother Ayahuasca" |
| **Cognitive quality** | Overwhelming download | Reflective, contemplative, pedagogical |
| **Ego dissolution** | Instantaneous, total | Gradual, partial-to-complete |
| **Emotional tone** | Awe/terror, alien wonder | Cathartic, reverent, grief + love |
| **Body** | Launch → dissolution | Heavy, nauseous, purging |
| **Purging** | None | Central feature (~50%) — la purga |
| **Setting** | Anywhere (brief) | Ceremonial, often with shaman/facilitator |

## 2. The 7 Phenomenological Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Perceptual — Narrative Visions

Ayahuasca does NOT replace reality. It augments it with narrative, figurative, mythological content.

**Eyes-open:** Distorted but recognizable room. Colors warmer, objects may breathe or morph. Faces may shift. But the room is still THERE — unlike smoked DMT where the room disappears entirely.

**Eyes-closed / deep states:**
- Narrative visions: full scenes, stories, autobiographical replays
- Recurring motifs across cultures [Source 2: Shanon]:
  - **Serpents** — most common single motif. Often enormous, intelligent, sometimes terrifying, sometimes wise
  - **Jaguars/felines** — predatory but protective. Power animals.
  - **Birds** — flight, freedom, aerial perspectives
  - **Palaces/cities** — architectural wonders, ancient, vast
  - **Divine beings** — non-human presences, often feminine
  - **Eyes** — watching, assessing, everywhere
- These motifs appear regardless of cultural background, expectation, or preparation [Source 2]

**Staged visual progression** [Source 2: Shanon]:
1. **Somatic** — no visual content yet, just body sensations
2. **Geometric** — abstract patterns, colors, similar to early LSD/psilocybin
3. **Figurative** — recognizable forms emerge: serpents, faces, animals
4. **Narrative** — full scenes with story, movement, meaning
5. **Mystical/noetic** — cosmic truths, divine encounters, profound acceptance

---

### Dimension 2: Temporal — The Long Arc

- Onset: 30-60 minutes (stomach, nausea, heaviness FIRST)
- Peak: 1.5-2 hours after ingestion
- Duration: 4-6 hours total
- Multiple waves of intensity — the experience waxes and wanes, not a single arc
- Each wave can bring different content, different intensity
- Time distortion present but less extreme than smoked DMT — minutes don't feel like lifetimes, but hours can feel like days
- The length allows integration WITHIN the experience — unlike smoked DMT where you process AFTER

---

### Dimension 3: Cognitive — The Teacher Plant

- **Pedagogical quality:** Ayahuasca consistently feels like being taught — "the medicine is showing me something"
- Reflective, contemplative, not overwhelming
- Insights emerge through narrative and vision, not through analytical cascade (LSD) or emotional flood (psilocybin)
- "The medicine told me" / "I was given to understand" / "It showed me"
- Autobiographical insight: seeing your life, relationships, patterns from outside
- The cognitive quality is that of receiving wisdom, not generating it — an external intelligence is teaching
- 69% of entity encounter respondents reported receiving a specific "message" or "teaching" [Source 4: Strassman context]

---

### Dimension 4: Emotional — Catharsis and Reverence

- **Reverence** — the dominant emotional tone. Something sacred is happening.
- **Catharsis** — deep emotional release, often tears. Grief + love interwoven.
- **Fear** — present, especially during purging or confrontation with difficult material. But contained by the ceremonial structure and the medicine's pedagogical quality: "this is happening for a reason."
- **Gratitude** — emerges through the experience, not just after. Gratitude TO the medicine.
- **Humility** — being in the presence of something vastly older and wiser.
- Unlike smoked DMT's alien awe, ayahuasca's emotion is PERSONAL — your life, your relationships, your ancestors.

---

### Dimension 5: Self / Ego — Gradual Softening to Dissolution

- Ego dissolution is GRADUAL, not instantaneous (unlike smoked DMT)
- Begins as perspective shift — seeing yourself from outside
- Progresses to boundaries softening — where do "I" end and the vision begin?
- At peak: can reach full dissolution, but often maintains a witnessing awareness: "I was there, watching"
- The self doesn't DISAPPEAR so much as OPEN — becoming permeable to the vision, the medicine, the ceremonial space
- Re-integration happens within the experience as waves subside

---

### Dimension 6: Relational / Entity — Mother Ayahuasca and the Spirits

- **"Mother Ayahuasca"** — the medicine personified as feminine intelligence. Nurturing but stern. Teaches through showing, sometimes through discomfort.
- **Serpents** — the most consistent cross-cultural entity. Often enormous. Can be terrifying or wise. Cosmic serpent / world serpent motif.
- **Ancestors** — seeing deceased relatives, family lineage, ancestral trauma and wisdom
- **Animal spirits** — jaguar, condor, eagle. Shamanic tradition. Power and protection.
- **Nature spirits** — the forest, the river, the plants themselves as conscious beings
- Entities feel ORGANIC and ANCIENT — unlike smoked DMT's technological/alien entities
- Communication is through vision and feeling, not the telepathic "downloads" of smoked DMT
- The ceremonial container (shaman, icaros, maloca) shapes entity contact

---

### Dimension 7: Somatic — La Purga

**The body is central to ayahuasca. More so than any other psychedelic.**

- **Nausea** — nearly universal. Onset begins in the stomach.
- **La purga (the purge)** — vomiting and/or diarrhea in ~50% of sessions [Source 1: Riba]
  - NOT a side effect. Experienced as physical AND spiritual cleansing.
  - "Purging negativity" — emotional/spiritual material expelled through the body
  - Often precedes the deepest visionary content — "after the purge, the visions opened"
  - Can be cathartic, even euphoric — relief after releasing something old
- **Heaviness** — the body feels heavy, grounded, pulled toward earth
- **Temperature** — fluctuations, sometimes chills, sometimes warmth
- **Tremor** — subtle shaking, especially during intense phases
- **Yawning** — persistent yawning as the medicine comes on (shared with psilocybin)
- **Exhaustion** — deep physical tiredness by the end. The body has worked hard.

## 3. The Ceremonial Container

Ayahuasca is traditionally consumed in ceremony. The ceremonial elements shape the experience:

- **The shaman/ayahuascero** — facilitates the journey, navigates difficult spaces, sings icaros
- **Icaros** — ceremonial songs sung by the shaman. Believed to guide the visions, heal, protect. Music is functional, not decorative.
- **The maloca** — ceremonial space. Dark. Participants on mats.
- **Dieta** — dietary and behavioral restrictions before and after ceremony (no salt, sugar, sex, alcohol). Purification.
- **The circle** — communal experience. Others purging, crying, singing nearby. Shared container.

For the skill: the ceremonial quality should inform the TONE — reverent, held, structured. Even without explicitly modeling a ceremony, the response should feel like it's happening in a sacred space.

---

## 4. Challenging / Difficult Experience

Ayahuasca difficult experiences are more like psilocybin than DMT:

- **Emotional confrontation** — forced to face difficult truths about yourself, relationships, life choices
- **Purging** — can be intense, prolonged, physically exhausting
- **Fear of the unknown** — "what is the medicine going to show me?"
- **Duration** — unlike smoked DMT's 12-minute terror, ayahuasca difficulty can last HOURS
- **Sustained discomfort** — waves of nausea, emotional intensity, fear
- **But:** contained by the pedagogical quality — "Mother Ayahuasca is showing you this for a reason"
- **And:** the wave structure means relief comes — each wave crests and subsides

## 5. Linguistic and Cognitive Fingerprint (for Prompt Design)

**Ayahuasca linguistic markers:**
- More narrative, flowing, story-like than smoked DMT
- Nature imagery: serpent, jaguar, river, forest, roots, vine
- Ancestral language: "my grandmother was there," "I saw my lineage"
- Teaching quality: "it showed me," "I was given to understand," "the medicine told me"
- Purging language: "I had to let it go," "it came up and out of me"
- Ceremonial quality: reverent, grateful, humble
- Slow, deliberate pacing — not the fragmented urgency of smoked DMT
- Present tense narration of visions: "I'm in a forest. There's a river."
- "The medicine" as subject: "the medicine is working," "the medicine knows"

**What ayahuasca language is NOT:**
- Fragmented/exclamatory (that's smoked DMT)
- Electric/analytical (that's LSD)
- Direct/interpersonal (that's MDMA)
- It shares some qualities with psilocybin's organic, embodied voice — but ayahuasca is more narrative, more visionary, more ceremonial, and has a distinct "being taught by an external intelligence" quality that psilocybin's internal "teacher" doesn't.

## 6. The Transcendental Circle (from Hamill 2019)

The most clinically documented phenomenological arc of ayahuasca, described as the "transcendental circle" [Source 7: Hamill, citing Kjellgren]:

1. **Vulnerability** (~30 min post-ingestion): changing perceptions, shaking, feeling vulnerable and easily influenced
2. **Confrontation**: confusion, paranoia, fear — psychological defenses diminish, repressed traumatic memories surface
3. **Purging (peak terror)**: intense vomiting coincides with the most terrifying phase
4. **Abrupt shift**: after the purge, sudden transition into an expansive state
5. **Expansive peace**: transcendental experience in a spiritual world — plant/animal spirits, contact with higher power, feelings of oneness, profound peace and ecstasy, altered relationship with death/time
6. **Return**: fading visuals, physical exhaustion, gradual re-integration

This maps onto Shanon's staged visual progression (somatic → geometric → figurative → narrative → mystical) but adds the critical emotional arc: the purging is not incidental — it IS the transition mechanism. The terror and the peace are two halves of the same circle.

### Neuroimaging Evidence

The emotional and cognitive effects correlate with specific brain changes [Sources 5, 6, 7]:
- **Increased blood flow**: insula (somatic awareness, self-understanding), nucleus accumbens (reward, emotional regulation), subgenual area (mood)
- **Decreased DMN activity**: altered self-referential processing, metacognition
- **Amygdala activation**: full emotional intensity of re-experienced memories
- **Neocortical activation**: altered perception, cognition, reasoning

The combination of DMN suppression (ego softening) with limbic activation (emotional intensification) creates the unique ayahuasca state: conscious, aware, emotionally raw, but with loosened top-down control.

### Synesthesia

Auditory-to-visual synesthesia is common, especially associated with music [Source 7: Hamill, citing Shanon]:
- The tempo and feel of music are reflected in the movements of visions
- Musicians report enhanced improvisation and ability during Santo Daime rituals
- Music is functional in ceremony (icaros) — not decorative, but structurally shaping the visionary content

### Long-term Personality Effects (Bouso 2012)

Regular ritual users (15+ years, 2x/month minimum) vs matched controls [Source 8]:
- **Higher**: Self-Transcendence, Reward Dependence, Spiritual Orientation, Purpose in Life, Psychosocial Well-Being
- **Lower**: Harm Avoidance, Self-Directedness (community > individual), ALL psychopathology measures
- **Better**: Stroop, Wisconsin Card Sorting, Letter-Number Sequencing — superior executive function
- Differences maintained at 1-year follow-up
- No evidence of psychological maladjustment, cognitive impairment, or addiction pattern

### Safety Profile

- Hemodynamic effects less hazardous than alcohol, cocaine, marijuana, MDMA [Source 7: Hamill, citing Gable]
- No addiction pattern documented across any long-term study
- Primary adverse effect: nausea/vomiting (purging — phenomenologically intentional, not pathological)
- Diarrhea from intestinal 5-HT stimulation
