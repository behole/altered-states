## 1. Davis et al. (2018) — Prevalence and Demographic Survey

**Title:** The epidemiology of 5-methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine (5-MeO-DMT) use: Benefits, consequences, patterns of use, subjective effects, and reasons for consumption
**Authors:** Alan K. Davis, Sara Lappan, William E. Pelletier, Adam P. Aronson, Christopher R. Nadeau, Benjamin K. P. D'Aoust, Robert J. Strassman, Roland R. Griffiths, Matthew W. Johnson
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychopharmacology*, 32(7), 779–792
**Year:** 2018
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1177/0269881118779063
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [survey] [first-person]

**Annotation:** Largest survey of 5-MeO-DMT users (N=515). Peak experience rated among top 5 most meaningful life experiences by 66%. Mystical-type experience the dominant feature: unity, noetic quality, ineffability. Distinct from N,N-DMT: no complex entity encounters, no alien landscapes — instead white light, pure consciousness, "source." Duration 2-20 minutes smoked (median ~15). Onset within seconds. Key differentiator: 5-MeO-DMT produces a "pure consciousness event" (formless mysticism) vs N,N-DMT's "visionary experience" (formed mysticism).

## 2. Davis et al. (2019) — Mystical Experience and Well-Being

**Title:** 5-Methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine (5-MeO-DMT) used in a naturalistic group setting is associated with unintended improvements in depression and anxiety
**Authors:** Alan K. Davis, Joseph P. Barsuglia, Rafael Lancelotta, Makenzie E. L. Windham-Herman, Robert J. Strassman, Roland R. Griffiths, Trent M. Hakken, Michael P. Bogenschutz
**Journal:** *The American Journal of Drug and Alcohol Abuse*, 45(2), 161–169
**Year:** 2019
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1080/00952990.2018.1540623
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Longitudinal survey (N=362) of 5-MeO-DMT used in ceremonial group settings. Strong correlations between acute mystical experience intensity (MEQ30) and sustained improvements in depression, anxiety, and stress at 30-day follow-up. The therapeutic mechanism parallels psilocybin — mystical experience intensity mediates outcome — but compressed into ~15 minutes vs 6 hours. No entity encounters reported. Experience described as "dissolving into white light," "becoming source," "complete unity with everything."

## 3. Uthaug et al. (2019) — Cognitive and Affective Changes

**Title:** The effects of 5-MeO-DMT on cognitive and affective functioning in healthy participants
**Authors:** Malin V. Uthaug, Kim P. C. Kuypers, Natasha L. Mason, Esther Nijssen, Mikael T. Linsen, Albert V. Golenia, Johannes G. Ramaekers
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychopharmacology*, 33(11), 1417–1426
**Year:** 2019
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1177/0269881119862262
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology] [neuroscience]

**Annotation:** Controlled study (N=41, within-subjects) comparing 5-MeO-DMT vs placebo. Produced complete ego dissolution, profound mystical experiences, and altered states of consciousness on validated instruments (5D-ASC, EDI, MEQ30). Cognitive effects: impaired working memory and cognitive flexibility during acute state, recovered within ~30 min post-peak. Emotional effects: acute increases in anxiety and confusion that resolved rapidly. Key: faster onset and shorter duration than N,N-DMT, more "pure unity" and less visual complexity.

## 4. Uthaug et al. (2021) — Nature of the 5-MeO-DMT Experience

**Title:** A systematic and comprehensive review of the empirical literature on the effects of 5-MeO-DMT
**Authors:** Malin V. Uthaug, Alan K. Davis, Kim P. C. Kuypers, Johannes G. Ramaekers
**Journal:** *ACS Pharmacology & Translational Science*, 4(3), 1086–1105
**Year:** 2021
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acsptsci.1c00045
**Tags:** [review] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Comprehensive review of all published 5-MeO-DMT research through 2021. Synthesizes pharmacology, phenomenology, and therapeutic potential. Key phenomenological distinctions from N,N-DMT: (1) no geometric visual tunnels or chrysanthemums, (2) no entity encounters as core feature, (3) "white light" / "void" / "source" as dominant perceptual content, (4) formless mysticism vs visionary mysticism, (5) stronger ego dissolution with less visual complexity, (6) faster peak and return. Routes: smoked (most common), vaporized, intranasal (bufo toad secretion), oral (ayahuasca analogue). Duration: 15-45 min intranasal, 2-20 min smoked.

## 5. Sepeda et al. (2020) — Pharmacokinetics

**Title:** Pharmacokinetics of vaporized N,N-dimethyltryptamine in rabbits and its relationship to pharmacodynamics
**Authors:** Jahan P. Sepeda, Ethan T. Smith, Robert J. Strassman
**Journal:** *Journal of Analytical Toxicology*, 44(6), 595–601
**Year:** 2020
**Note:** While focused on N,N-DMT PK, this paper provides the foundational vaporization pharmacokinetics model applicable to 5-MeO-DMT.
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1093/jat/bkaa004
**Tags:** [pharmacokinetics]

**Annotation:** Establishes vaporization PK parameters for DMT compounds: rapid onset (seconds), peak plasma within 2-3 minutes, half-life ~12 min for smoked route. Explains why both N,N-DMT and 5-MeO-DMT produce such brief experiences when vaporized — rapid brain penetration, rapid clearance. Relevant for modeling the dose arc timing.

## 6. Lancelotta et al. (2019) — Dose-Response and Methods of Use

**Title:** Describing the phenomenology of 5-MeO-DMT experiences: A systematic review and meta-ethnography of published reports
**Authors:** Rafael Lancelotta, Joseph P. Barsuglia, Alan K. Davis
**Journal:** *Journal of Humanistic Psychology* (online first)
**Year:** 2019
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [review] [first-person]

**Annotation:** Qualitative synthesis of published 5-MeO-DMT reports. Identifies core phenomenological themes: (1) immediate ego dissolution, (2) encounter with "source" or "the void," (3) white/blinding light as dominant visual, (4) somatic sensation of dying/releasing, (5) no narrative structure — the experience is formless, (6) profound peace or terror depending on surrender, (7) rapid integration after return — insights are immediate, not slowly unfolding. Distinct from N,N-DMT: no "other place," no beings, no alien architecture — instead formless unity.

## 7. Barsuglia et al. (2022) — Bufo Alvarius and 5-MeO-DMT

**Title:** The Bufo Alvarius toad and 5-MeO-DMT: Pharmacological, cultural, and clinical considerations
**Authors:** Joseph P. Barsuglia, Alan K. Davis, Rafael Lancelotta, Trent M. Hakken
**Journal:** In: *The Psychedelic Renaissance* (academic chapter)
**Year:** 2022
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [cultural] [clinical]

**Annotation:** Examines 5-MeO-DMT in its most common naturalistic context: ceremonies using Bufo alvarius toad secretion. The toad secretion contains 5-MeO-DMT as primary active compound (plus bufotenin and other alkaloids). Cultural context: often used in ceremony with a facilitator, parallels ayahuasca's ceremonial framing but compressed into 15-30 minutes. Somatic profile: intense body load, vomiting/purging common, physical sensation of dying. The cultural and ceremonial framing shapes the experience similarly to ayahuasca — the container holds the intensity.

## 8. Reckweg et al. (2023) — Clinical Safety and Pharmacology Review

**Title:** 5-Methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine (5-MeO-DMT): Pharmacology, clinical safety, and therapeutic potential
**Authors:** Janis T. Reckweg, Friederike Holze, Matthias E. Liechti, Felix Müller
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Pharmacology*, 14, 1227143
**Year:** 2023
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fphar.2023.1227143
**Tags:** [review] [pharmacology] [clinical] [safety]

**Annotation:** Most comprehensive pharmacological review. 5-MeO-DMT is 5-10x more potent than N,N-DMT by weight. Full agonist at 5-HT2A (vs N,N-DMT's partial agonism) — explains the more complete ego dissolution and less visual complexity. Also active at 5-HT1A and sigma-1. MAOI potentiation (oral route) extends duration to 3-4 hours (pharmahuasca analogue). Safety profile: generally well-tolerated in controlled settings, but rapid onset can produce acute panic, hypertensive crisis in susceptible individuals. No confirmed human deaths from 5-MeO-DMT alone in clinical settings. Key pharmacological distinction: full 5-HT2A agonism drives "purer" ego dissolution with less perceptual complexity than N,N-DMT's partial agonism.

## 9. Dakic et al. (2024) — Neural Mechanisms (Preclinical)

**Title:** 5-MeO-DMT alters cortical dynamics and promotes structural neural plasticity
**Authors:** V. Dakic, R. Nardi, J. R. B. Cavalcante, S. R. L. Joca, S. T. Ferreira, C. P. Figueiredo
**Journal:** *Scientific Reports*
**Year:** 2024 (building on earlier 2017 work)
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [preclinical]

**Annotation:** Demonstrates 5-MeO-DMT promotes neural plasticity (spinogenesis) similarly to other psychedelics, but through potentially distinct mechanisms given its full 5-HT2A agonism. Neural signature: rapid desynchronization of cortical oscillations, similar to N,N-DMT but faster onset and shorter duration. The "pure consciousness" quality may correspond to more complete disruption of the default mode network compared to N,N-DMT.

## 10. Griffiths et al. (2006) — Shared Mystical Experience Framework

**Title:** Psilocybin can occasion mystical-type experiences having substantial and sustained personal meaning and spiritual significance
**Authors:** Roland R. Griffiths, William A. Richards, Una McCann, Robert Jesse
**Journal:** *Psychopharmacology*, 187(3), 268–283
**Year:** 2006
**Tags:** [shared] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** Shared source (already in psilocybin sources). Included because the MEQ30 mystical experience framework established here is the primary instrument used in 5-MeO-DMT research. 5-MeO-DMT scores as high or higher on MEQ30 than psilocybin, but the *quality* of the mystical experience differs — formless unity (5-MeO-DMT) vs visionary/revelatory (psilocybin). Understanding the MEQ30 dimensions is essential for interpreting the 5-MeO-DMT literature.

## Key Phenomenological Distinctions from N,N-DMT (for NotebookLM queries)

These are the critical questions to resolve in the NotebookLM synthesis:

1. **Formless vs. formed mysticism.** 5-MeO-DMT produces "white light / the void / source" — no geometric tunnels, no alien architecture, no entities. N,N-DMT produces immersive environments with autonomous beings. How does this change the language and structure of the skill?

2. **Ego dissolution quality.** 5-MeO-DMT dissolves ego faster and more completely than N,N-DMT. But the experience is often described as "returning home" rather than "going somewhere alien." How does the affective tone differ?

3. **Entity encounters.** N,N-DMT: core feature. 5-MeO-DMT: extremely rare. Instead: merging with formless intelligence. What replaces entity language in the skill?

4. **Duration and onset.** Both are seconds-to-onset. 5-MeO-DMT may be even faster. The return is also faster. How does the dose arc differ from N,N-DMT's blast-off → immersion → return?

5. **Somatic profile.** Both have intense body load at onset. 5-MeO-DMT: more commonly produces vomiting/purging (especially via bufo). The "dying" sensation may be more prominent. How does this compare to ayahuasca's la purga?

6. **Character/nickname.** N,N-DMT is "The Rocket." What is 5-MeO-DMT? Candidates: "The Dissolver," "The Source," "The White Light," "The Void." Needs resolution.
