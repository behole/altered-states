## 1. Mithoefer et al. (2018) — MAPS Phase 2 PTSD Trial

**Title:** 3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine (MDMA)-assisted psychotherapy for post-traumatic stress disorder in military veterans, firefighters, and police officers: a randomised, double-blind, dose-response, phase 2 clinical trial
**Authors:** Michael C. Mithoefer, Ann T. Mithoefer, Allison A. Feduccia, Lisa Jerome, Mark Wagner, et al., Rick Doblin
**Journal:** *The Lancet Psychiatry*, 5(6), 486–497
**Year:** 2018
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1016/S2215-0366(18)30135-4
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Pivotal Phase 2 RCT (N=26) in treatment-resistant PTSD. Three dose groups: 30mg (active placebo), 75mg, 125mg. MDMA's therapeutic mechanism: reduces fear while maintaining cognitive coherence, enabling trauma processing within expanded "window of tolerance." 75mg and 125mg groups showed significantly greater PTSD improvement. Used 5D-ASC showing HIGH emotional/social effects with LOW perceptual and ego dissolution effects.

## 2. Mitchell et al. (2021) — Phase 3 Trial (Nature Medicine)

**Title:** MDMA-assisted therapy for severe PTSD: a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 study
**Authors:** Jennifer M. Mitchell, Michael Bogenschutz, et al., Rick Doblin
**Journal:** *Nature Medicine*, 27, 1025–1033
**Year:** 2021
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-021-01336-3
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** Landmark Phase 3 (N=90, severe PTSD). 67% of MDMA group no longer met PTSD criteria at 2-month follow-up vs 32% placebo (d=0.91). Three 8-hour sessions with split doses (80-120mg + optional 40-60mg booster). Validated the therapeutic model: MDMA creates a window for trauma processing through fear reduction and emotional openness, with maintained cognitive coherence and enhanced therapeutic alliance. No hallucinations reported.

## 3. Nichols (1986/2004) — Entactogen Classification

**Title:** Differences Between the Mechanism of Action of MDMA, MBDB, and the Classic Hallucinogens. Identification of a New Therapeutic Class: Entactogens
**Authors:** David E. Nichols
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychoactive Drugs*, 18(4), 305–313
**Year:** 1986
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1080/02791072.1986.10472362
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** Foundational paper coining "entactogen" (from Greek/Latin: "touching within") to taxonomically distinguish MDMA from hallucinogens. MDMA works via transporter reversal (monoamine release), NOT 5-HT2A agonism. Does not produce hallucinations or substitute for LSD in drug discrimination assays. Defines the entactogenic syndrome: emotional openness, empathy, self-insight, fear reduction, with preserved cognitive coherence and intact ego structure.

## 4. Hysek, Domes & Liechti (2012) — Empathy Enhancement

**Title:** MDMA enhances emotional empathy and prosocial behavior
**Authors:** C.M. Hysek, G. Domes, M.E. Liechti
**Journal:** *Social Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience*, 7(6), 645–652
**Year:** 2012
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1093/scan/nsr051
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** N=30, 125mg MDMA, placebo-controlled. Key dissociation: MDMA enhances EMOTIONAL empathy (feeling with others) but NOT cognitive empathy (intellectually understanding emotions). Elevated plasma oxytocin provides neuroendocrine mechanism for bonding and trust. Increased prosocial behavior on resource allocation tasks. The empathic effect is bottom-up emotional resonance, not top-down perspective-taking.

## 5. Liechti, Gamma & Vollenweider (2000/2001) — 5D-ASC Pharmacology

**Title:** Acute Effects of MDMA are Attenuated by the Serotonin Uptake Inhibitor Citalopram / Dose-response study of MDMA on subjective effects
**Authors:** Matthias E. Liechti, Alex Gamma, Franz X. Vollenweider
**Journal:** *Neuropsychopharmacology* / *Psychopharmacology*
**Year:** 2000/2001
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1016/S0893-133X(99)00148-7
**Tags:** [clinical] [dose-response] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** First quantitative 5D-ASC comparison of MDMA against classic psychedelics. MDMA scored ~1/3 to 1/4 of psilocybin on Visionary Restructuralization, much lower on Anxious Ego Dissolution, but comparably elevated on Blissful State and interpersonal closeness. Citalopram blocked MDMA's effects, confirming serotonin release (via SERT reversal) as primary mechanism. Established the inverted phenomenological profile: HIGH emotional, LOW perceptual.

## 6. Schmid, Hysek & Liechti (2014) — Subjective and Somatic Effects

**Title:** Acute Effects of MDMA on Subjective Experiences, Empathy, and Autonomic Function in Healthy Volunteers
**Authors:** Yasmin Schmid, C.M. Hysek, L.D. Simmler, M.J. Crockett, B.B. Quednow, M.E. Liechti
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychopharmacology*, 28(8), 727–739
**Year:** 2014
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1177/0269881114531776
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** Comprehensive somatic and subjective effects profile. Tactile enhancement unique to MDMA — touch becomes pleasurable and emotionally meaningful. Bruxism in 70-90% of participants. Body temperature elevated 0.3-1.0°C. The "rolling" sensation: pulsatile waves of euphoria. Cardiovascular: HR +20-30 bpm, BP +25mmHg systolic. Emotional warmth experienced somatically — in the chest, skin, face.

## 7. Holze, Vizeli & Liechti (2020) — MDMA vs LSD vs Amphetamine

**Title:** Distinct acute effects of LSD, MDMA, and d-amphetamine in healthy subjects
**Authors:** Friederike Holze, Patrick Vizeli, Felix Müller, Laura Ley, et al., Matthias E. Liechti
**Journal:** *Neuropsychopharmacology*, 45, 462–471
**Year:** 2020
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41386-019-0569-3
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Head-to-head comparison in same participants. MDMA produced significantly more "closeness to others," "trust," and "desire to be with others" than LSD or d-amphetamine. LSD produced dramatically more perceptual effects. MDMA's empathogenic profile is distinct from both psychedelic (LSD) and stimulant (amphetamine) classes. Confirms MDMA occupies unique pharmacological niche: simultaneously stimulant + empathogen + mild psychedelic, but NOT hallucinogen.

## 8. Passie & Benzenhöfer (2016) — Comprehensive Pharmacology Review

**Title:** The Pharmacology of MDMA in Humans
**Authors:** Torsten Passie, Udo Benzenhöfer
**Journal:** *Therapeutic Advances in Psychopharmacology* (and related reviews)
**Year:** 2016
**Tags:** [neuroscience] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Comprehensive review synthesizing >50 controlled studies. Establishes the "entactogenic syndrome": euphoria, emotional warmth, empathy, increased sociability, reduced fear, increased self-acceptance, emotional clarity. Key finding: MDMA's effects are MORE PREDICTABLE AND UNIFORM than psychedelic effects — most users report similar core emotional experience, vs high variability with LSD/psilocybin. This reliability is therapeutically valuable.

## 9. Curran, Parrott et al. (2000-2013) — Comedown and Depletion

**Title:** The "Ecstasy Hangover" / MDMA Serotonergic Neurotoxicity / Diverse Functional Deficits
**Authors:** H. Valerie Curran, Andrew C. Parrott (various)
**Journal:** *Psychopharmacology*, *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews*
**Year:** 2000-2013
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2013.04.016
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [clinical]

**Annotation:** Documents the unique MDMA comedown — no parallel in classic psychedelics. "Tuesday Blues": depressive episode 2-5 days post-use, peaking day 3, from serotonin depletion. The comedown reverses acute effects: where MDMA opened the heart, the comedown constricts it. Users describe feeling "raw," "exposed," "fragile." Classic psychedelics don't deplete serotonin (they agonize receptors), so they produce afterglow rather than crash. The depletion arc is integral to MDMA phenomenology.

## 10. de la Torre et al. (2004) — Pharmacokinetics

**Title:** Pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics of MDMA and its metabolites in healthy volunteers
**Authors:** Rafael de la Torre, Magí Farré, Pere N. Roset, et al.
**Journal:** *Therapeutic Drug Monitoring*, 26(2), 137–144
**Year:** 2004
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1097/00007691-200404000-00009
**Tags:** [clinical] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Hard pharmacokinetic numbers. Onset: 20-30min. Tmax: 1.5-2.5h. Half-life: 7-9h (but effects fade earlier due to serotonin depletion — "run out of serotonin before running out of drug"). CRITICAL: nonlinear pharmacokinetics via CYP2D6 auto-inhibition — doubling dose more than doubles plasma level. MDA (active metabolite, more psychedelic) accumulates late in session. Therapeutic booster protocol (half-dose at T+1.5-2.5h) extends plateau without spiking peak concentration.
