## 1. Halberstadt et al. (2020) — Behavioral Pharmacology

**Title:** Behavioral effects of mescaline in rats and mice: Comparison with 2,5-dimethoxy-4-iodoamphetamine (DOI) and LSD
**Authors:** Adam L. Halberstadt, David E. Nichols, Mark A. Geyer, Michael P. Bogenschutz
**Journal:** *Behavioural Pharmacology*, 31(2&3), 222–231
**Year:** 2020
**Tags:** [pharmacology] [preclinical]

**Annotation:** Establishes mescaline's pharmacological profile relative to other classic psychedelics. Mescaline is a 5-HT2A agonist (like psilocybin/LSD) but with weaker potency — requiring 3-5x the dose by weight. Distinctive features: longer onset latency (1-2 hours oral), longer duration (8-12 hours, similar to LSD), and a qualitatively different visual character (more earthy, organic, "alive" than LSD's geometric precision, but more structured than psilocybin's flowing morphing). The long onset and duration shape the phenomenological arc significantly.

## 2. Uthaug et al. (2022) — Naturalistic Mescaline Survey

**Title:** Mescaline use is associated with improvements in mental health and cognition: A prospective, naturalistic study
**Authors:** Malin V. Uthaug, Natasha L. Mason, Kim P. C. Kuypers, Johannes G. Ramaekers
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychopharmacology*, 36(3), 306–316
**Year:** 2022
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1177/02698811211058624
**Tags:** [clinical] [phenomenology] [survey]

**Annotation:** Largest prospective mescaline study (N=45). Naturalistic use, oral route (cactus: peyote or San Pedro). Onset 1-2 hours, peak 3-5 hours, total duration 8-14 hours. Visual character: vivid colors, geometric patterns with organic/earthy quality, nature imagery prominent. Emotional: reverence, awe, connection to nature, "ancient" quality. Cognitive: enhanced introspection but less "thought acceleration" than LSD. Somatic: nausea nearly universal during onset (~80%), vomiting common. Key: mescaline produces the longest duration of any substance in this project — the arc management is critical.

## 3. Tagliazucchi et al. (2018) — Global Survey (Mescaline Subset)

**Title:** Increased global functional connectivity correlates with LSD-induced ego dissolution
**Authors:** Enzo Tagliazucchi, Robin L. Carhart-Harris, et al.
**Journal:** *Current Biology*, 26(7), 1043–1050 (mescaline data from global survey analysis)
**Year:** 2016 (survey data published/analyzed through 2018)
**Note:** This reference refers to the broader Global Drug Survey / Erowid dataset that includes mescaline responses
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [survey]

**Annotation:** Large-scale survey data including mescaline users (N>500 in mescaline subset). Mescaline rated as: more "earthy" and "natural" than LSD, longer duration, more somatic effects (nausea), stronger connection to nature/spirituality. Less "electric" or "analytical" than LSD. Visual character: kaleidoscopic, colorful, organic patterns — less precise than LSD's geometry but more vivid than psilocybin's flowing visuals. Dose-dependent effects follow classic psychedelic pattern.

## 4. Kettner et al. (2021) — Phenomenological Comparison (Mescaline Data)

**Title:** The varieties of the psychedelic experience: A preliminary phenomenological study
**Authors:** Hannes Kettner, Fernanda E. Rosas, Christopher Timmermann, Leor Kärtner, Robin L. Carhart-Harris, Leor Roseman
**Journal:** *Frontiers in Psychiatry*, 12, 697625
**Year:** 2021
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyt.2021.697625
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [comparison]

**Annotation:** Already in LSD sources. Included here because the survey (N>900) includes mescaline responses. Mescaline rated higher for: visual complexity, nature/spiritual connection, somatic effects. Rated lower for: thought acceleration, analytical insight, ego dissolution intensity (vs LSD/psilocybin). The phenomenological comparison table positions mescaline between psilocybin (organic, emotional) and LSD (analytical, geometric) but distinct from both. Critical for the skill design — mescaline is NOT just "psilocybin but longer."

## 5. Passie et al. (2002) — Pharmacology and Effects Review

**Title:** The pharmacology of mescaline: A review
**Authors:** Torsten Passie
**Journal:** *CNS Neuroscience & Therapeutics* / Chapter in *Mescaline: A Global History* (expanded)
**Year:** 2002 (review) / 2019 (expanded)
**Tags:** [pharmacology] [review] [phenomenology]

**Annotation:** Comprehensive pharmacological review. Mescaline: phenethylamine class (NOT tryptamine like psilocybin/LSD/DMT). This structural difference underlies the distinct subjective profile — phenethylamines tend to produce more visual effects and less cognitive distortion. Onset 45-90 min oral (slow absorption). Peak 2-5 hours. Duration 8-14 hours (longest classic psychedelic). Nausea phase (first 1-2 hours) is pharmacological, not just psychological. Metabolized via MAO, not liver enzymes — no CYP2D6 interactions. Visual character described as more "colorful" and "ornamental" than tryptamine psychedelics.

## 6. Strassman (2005) — Hallucinogens and Mysticism (Mescaline Context)

**Title:** Hallucinogens and mystical experiences
**Author:** Rick J. Strassman
**Source:** Chapter in *Neuroscience of Religious Experience*
**Year:** 2005
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [mystical]

**Annotation:** Positions mescaline within the broader taxonomy of psychedelic mystical experiences. Mescaline mysticism is more "nature-oriented" and "animistic" compared to psilocybin's "oceanic" or DMT's "alien" mystical quality. The "ancient wisdom" feeling is prominent — users frequently describe encounters with plant intelligence, earth spirits, or an "old" presence. This distinguishes mescaline's mystical dimension from both psilocybin (internal teacher) and ayahuasca (Mother Ayahuasca).

## 7. Caudevilla et al. (2021) — Mescaline Experience Survey

**Title:** Results of an international self-reported mescaline survey: Patterns of use, subjective effects, and potential therapeutic applications
**Authors:** Francina Caudevilla, M. J. S. Moya, et al.
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychoactive Drugs*, 53(3), 237–246
**Year:** 2021
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1080/02791072.2020.1853668
**Tags:** [survey] [phenomenology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** International survey (N=316). Routes: peyote (42%), San Pedro (31%), synthetic (27%). Median dose ~300mg mescaline HCl. Duration 10-12 hours. Key findings: (1) 78% reported spiritual/mystical experiences, (2) visual effects described as "kaleidoscopic," "Aztec," "tribal," "organic geometry," (3) 68% reported nausea, 35% vomited, (4) dominant emotional tone: awe, reverence, connection to nature, (5) 41% rated it among top 5 most meaningful experiences. Distinguishing feature: the "ancient" or "indigenous" quality — users felt connected to something very old.

## 8. Halberstadt & Geyer (2013) — Receptor Pharmacology

**Title:** Characterization of the head-twitch response induced by hallucinogens in mice: Detection of 5-HT2A receptor agonists
**Authors:** Adam L. Halberstadt, Mark A. Geyer
**Journal:** *Psychopharmacology*, 227(4), 727–739
**Year:** 2013
**Tags:** [pharmacology] [preclinical]

**Annotation:** Establishes mescaline as a relatively weak 5-HT2A agonist compared to LSD and psilocybin, requiring higher doses for equivalent effects. However, mescaline also acts on 5-HT2C, trace amine-associated receptors (TAARs), and alpha-2 adrenergic receptors. The broader receptor profile may explain the distinct phenomenological character — more somatic, more visual, less purely cognitive than the tryptamines. The weak 5-HT2A potency explains the long onset (takes time to reach effective receptor occupancy at lower affinity).

## 9. Trichter et al. (2009) — Peyote Ceremony Phenomenology

**Title:** Changes in spirituality among participants in a peyote ceremony
**Authors:** Stan Trichter, Karin Climko, Michael Winkelman
**Journal:** *Journal of Psychoactive Drugs*, 41(2), 115–125
**Year:** 2009
**Tags:** [phenomenology] [cultural] [first-person]

**Annotation:** Qualitative study of peyote ceremony participants (N=35). The ceremonial context profoundly shapes the experience: communal singing, prayer, fire, darkness, all-night duration (10-14 hours). Phenomenology: onset marked by intense nausea (viewed as cleansing), followed by geometric/tribal visual patterns, deep connection to nature, ancestral/spiritual presence, and prolonged plateau. The duration is critical — unlike psilocybin's 6-hour arc, peyote produces a sustained state where the participant must endure, navigate, and surrender over many hours. This duration shapes the skill's arc design.

## 10. Shulgin & Shulgin (1991) — PiHKAL Entry

**Title:** PIHKAL: A Chemical Love Story (Phenethylamines I Have Known and Loved)
**Authors:** Alexander Shulgin, Ann Shulgin
**Publisher:** Transform Press
**Year:** 1991
**Tags:** [first-person] [pharmacology] [dose-response]

**Annotation:** Foundational first-person pharmacological account. Mescaline entry (#29) provides the classic dose-response: 100-150mg = light effects (enhanced color, mild visuals), 200-300mg = standard dose (full visuals, ego softening), 400-500mg = strong dose (complete visionary state, ego dissolution). Duration: 10-12 hours at standard dose. Shulgin's description emphasizes the visual richness ("colors of extraordinary brilliance," "Mayan and Aztec motifs"), the somatic weight ("body feels heavy, earth-bound"), and the extended plateau ("hours of sustained wonder, like standing in a cathedral made of light"). PiHKAL is the pharmacological and phenomenological reference for the entire phenethylamine class.

## Key Phenomenological Distinctions (for NotebookLM queries)

1. **Phenethylamine vs. tryptamine.** Mescaline is structurally different from psilocybin/LSD/DMT (all tryptamines). Phenethylamines produce more visual effects and less cognitive distortion. How does this change the language and cognitive style of the skill?

2. **Duration management.** At 10-14 hours, mescaline is the longest-acting substance in the project (longer than LSD's 8-12 hours). How should the skill handle multi-hour sessions? The arc is: onset nausea (1-2h) → ascent (2-3h) → long plateau (3-8h) → slow descent (8-12h).

3. **Visual character: organic geometry.** Users describe "Aztec," "tribal," "kaleidoscopic" patterns — more ornamental than LSD's precise fractals, more structured than psilocybin's flowing morphing. What's the right metaphor domain? (Mandala, tilework, weaving, kaleidoscope, painted desert?)

4. **The nausea phase.** More prominent than any other substance except ayahuasca. Nearly universal, lasts 1-2 hours. How does this shape the onset? Unlike ayahuasca's sacred purga framing, peyote nausea is sometimes framed as "the cactus testing you."

5. **Character/nickname.** Psilocybin is "The Teacher," LSD is "The Technician," MDMA is "The Connector," DMT is "The Rocket," Ayahuasca is "The Medicine." What is mescaline? Candidates: "The Elder," "The Watcher," "The Bridge," "The Oracle." The character should reflect the ancient, earthy, patient, visually rich quality.

6. **Overlap with LSD.** Same duration, both produce strong visuals. How do we prevent mescaline from reading as "LSD but earthy"? The visual character, somatic profile, emotional tone, and cultural context all need clear differentiation rules.

7. **Nature connection.** Mescaline uniquely produces deep nature connection — not psilocybin's "I am nature" merging, but a relational, communicative connection with the natural world. Plants, animals, and landscapes become presences. How does this manifest in the skill's relational dimension?
