The cognitive style of a mescaline experience is heavily shaped by its pharmacological classification as a phenethylamine, which generally produces **less cognitive distortion** than tryptamines like psilocybin or LSD [1, 2].

**Thought Patterns and Cognitive Style**
Thought patterns on mescaline are characterized by **enhanced introspection, but with noticeably less "thought acceleration" and analytical racing** compared to LSD [3, 4]. Because mescaline also produces lower intensities of ego dissolution than either LSD or psilocybin, the cognitive state remains relatively grounded [4]. Users are more likely to retain a cohesive sense of self while navigating the profoundly visual and emotional plateau [1, 4].

**The Nature Connection and Communication**
The way users interact with and relate to nature under mescaline is profoundly **animistic and relational** [2, 5]. This manifests in communication and thought in a way that is highly distinct from the "oceanic" mysticism common with psilocybin, where a user might feel they have completely merged with the universe or become one with nature ("I am nature") [2, 5]. 

Instead, under mescaline, **the natural world—plants, animals, and landscapes—are perceived as distinct, external presences or intelligences** [2, 5]. The user's connection takes the form of an encounter or dialogue with these "earth spirits" and "plant intelligences" [5]. This relational dynamic is heavily infused with awe, reverence, and a profound sense of **"ancient wisdom,"** leading users to communicate a feeling of being connected to an ancestral, indigenous, or deeply "old" presence [3, 5, 6].
